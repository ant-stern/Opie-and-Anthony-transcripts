SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with Sean Mullins and Anthony recounting their experience at a John Mellencamp concert at the Bowery Ballroom the previous night.  Mullins recounted a brief interaction with Mellencamp's wife.  The hosts then announced their plan for a live broadcast from Tower Records in Lincoln Square during an in-store appearance by Mellencamp, encouraging listener participation.  They reviewed listener faxes, one suggesting a broadcast from a giant Pop-Tart, an idea they dismissed.  Further ludicrous broadcast location suggestions, including a giant strudel, bagel, and even a vagina, were jokingly floated and rejected.  A caller, Ed, confirmed their suspicions about the "world's largest Pop-Tart" being a promotional stunt composed of multiple individual Pop-Tarts.  The hosts then addressed a listener email criticizing their show and comparing them unfavorably to other radio personalities, leading to a discussion about listener predictions of their impending firing.  Before concluding the segment, they reminisced about watching an old episode of "Real People," mocking several segments, including a particularly tragic story about a man dying of leukemia before completing his self-built Viking ship. They also satirized various infomercials they had seen, notably one for a "workout stick" and a bird-song clock. The show ended with the hosts preparing to depart for Tower Records, playfully suggesting they might arrive on a small school bus wearing hockey helmets.
