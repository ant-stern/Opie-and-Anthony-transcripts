SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast began with a pre-show hosted by Sam Roberts, paying tribute to the recently deceased comedian and ventriloquist Otto Peterson.  Studio guests Bob Kelly and Jim Florentine, along with a phone call from David Copperfield, shared anecdotes about Otto, including a memorable incident where his puppet George was stabbed.  The pre-show also touched upon the upcoming "Unmasked" event with Ron Bennington, featuring pre-signed posters which sparked some disagreement regarding the rushed meet-and-greet format.  Listener calls and interactions with the unusually grumpy E-Rock (aka Sex Bagel, aka Hawk) dominated a significant portion of the pre-show.  Jim Norton's surprising lack of musical knowledge, the hosts' perspectives on parenting, and Courtney Love's criticism of saxophones in rock were also discussed.  Sal successfully pranked E-Rock into believing he was a caller.  Technical problems and the absence of Anthony and Travis due to illness concluded the pre-show, leading to further discussion about the limited-edition "Unmasked" posters.

The main show commenced with Opie, Anthony, and Sam Roberts criticizing the design of the "OA20" 20th-anniversary poster, noting its small size and the prominence of various comedians like Sam Kinison,  Bobby Kelly, Burr, Patrice Attell, Louis C.K., Fallon, John Cena, Guy Fieri, and Hulk Hogan, while their staff, especially E-Rock, received minimal representation.  The discussion devolved into a debate about the poster's limited edition status, with Opie and Anthony advocating for wider distribution.  Listener calls, including one from a fan whose husband disliked the show, punctuated the segment, along with digressions about a Chip and Uncle Paul podcast, the health of show personnel, technical issues, and the upcoming "Unmasked" show.  Rich Voss was mentioned as an upcoming guest; eventually Sam agreed to increase poster availability.  Further planning for the "Unmasked" show, a potential larger future event, and Rich Voss's upcoming appearance and stand-up gigs concluded this segment.

The next segment included numerous commercial breaks for Lumosity, WeatherTech, and other products, interspersed with a discussion about Otto Peterson. Rich Vos, a friend of Otto, joined in studio, sharing anecdotes about Otto's "filthy dirty" comedic style, drug use (cocaine and heroin), and his relationship with George the dummy.  He recounted stories involving Chris Rock and other comedians, and detailed Otto's interactions with audiences.  Colin Quinn called in, adding his own Otto stories.  A clip from a podcast featuring Jim Norton’s ex-girlfriend, Jennifer, interviewing Chip Chipperson, was played, revealing explicit details of their past relationship. More commercial breaks followed.

A lengthy and rambling conversation with Chip followed. The segment began with Chip unknowingly recounting graphic details about his mother's sexual activities before discussing his disappointing childhood birthday party.  The conversation then shifted to a man in Boston who placed a backpack near the marathon finish line (Kayvon Edson), causing a brief evacuation.  The hosts expressed disgust at Edson's actions.  The show then promoted upcoming events and took listener calls, interspersed with discussions about the Boston incident and a viral video of a Southwest Airlines flight attendant's inappropriate safety announcement.  The hosts and callers debated the flight attendant's performance.

The discussion regarding the flight attendant continued with Rich Voss expressing his disapproval, viewing it as an insult to radio personalities, while others defended her humor and potential.  Callers shared varying opinions. The segment then focused on viral videos: Sweet Brown's "Ain't nobody got time for that" video and a "car guy" YouTube video with the catchphrase "Would you look at that?"  Christopher Maloney and Dean Norris from the movie "Small Time" joined the show, discussing the film, their careers (including Norris’ *Breaking Bad* role), and used car salesmen. A caller shared a revenge story about a customer's leather gloves.  The segment concluded with further discussion about the viral videos and the guests.

A segment heavily featured Rich Voss recounting an awkward encounter with Dean Norris, repeatedly failing to remember the name of the movie (*The Counselor*) they met at.  Banter among the hosts, listener calls promoting Rich Voss's stand-up shows and an Otto podcast, a $44 million blue painting, a listener's complaint about Voss's interaction with a flight attendant, more discussion of Otto's death, and the mention of Dominic Ancel's new Easter treat filled the segment, punctuated by numerous commercial breaks.  The broadcast concluded without any further summarized segments.
