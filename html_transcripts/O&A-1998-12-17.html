SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a discussion of the Iraq War, specifically the bombing of Baghdad.  The hosts, Opie and Anthony, used crude humor to comment on CNN's war coverage, focusing on the "big penis building" and speculating about Saddam Hussein's location.  Their commentary also included sexually explicit jokes and references to the Clinton impeachment scandal.  They played listener faxes, one requesting a Scud missile be launched at them, and boasted about accurately predicting the Baghdad bombing in a previous segment.  Musical interludes punctuated the show, and they promoted their recently released CD, "Opie and Anthony's Demented World," highlighting its controversial parody of "Grandma Got Run Over by a Reindeer."

Red Peters, a musician and friend of the hosts, joined them in the studio.  He promoted his own CD, "I Laughed, I Cried, I Fudged My Undies," and performed songs live, including "How's Your Whole Family" and the explicitly titled "Blow Me."  The conversation included discussions of Restaurant Records, their record label, and its owner "Owen Money," along with numerous commercial sponsorships, such as a cigar-of-the-month club and Super Libido, a male enhancement supplement which Peters was apparently using on-air.  The hosts also discussed their frustrations with their record label representative, who showed up with only promotional t-shirts instead of addressing the low sales of their CD.  The discussion frequently devolved into sexually suggestive and crude topics, including references to transvestites and the John Wayne Bobbitt incident.

Peters continued his musical performance with the sexually suggestive song "You Promised the Moon, But I Preferred Uranus."  The hosts then engaged in humorous speculation about Peters' fictional collaborations with various musicians and singers, all with obscene names.  Their conversation shifted to a recollection of their trip to Atlanta during Freaknik, describing it as a chaotic and potentially intimidating experience for white people.  The broadcast concluded with a call from "Lamb Chop," the sock puppet, who, in character, used highly offensive language to express bitterness towards his deceased partner and recount a negative experience filming a Hanukkah special.  The show also advertised an upcoming show featuring Hank the Angry Drunken Dwarf and a Black Crows giveaway.
