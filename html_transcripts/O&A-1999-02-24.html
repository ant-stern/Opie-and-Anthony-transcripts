SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with Opie comparing the slow upload of pictures to their website to Anne Frank hiding, setting a tone of irreverent humor.  They then addressed the fallout from Opie's on-air mistake of confusing Thelma and Florida Evans, playing listener calls expressing their outrage.  A caller's anecdote about using a motorized shopping cart led to a humorous segment about an elderly "Little Rascal Race" and a discussion of generational differences, referencing the Cold War, Jay Leno's tenure on the Tonight Show, and nostalgic memories of 8-track tapes, Atari, and the Betamax/VHS format war.

The hosts heavily promoted Stephen Lynch's upcoming performance at Caroline's, contrasting it with a discussion about the changing fortunes of bands they once supported, like the Goo Goo Dolls, whose success had distanced them.  They then skewered MTV's "Fanatic," mocking the show's premise and the overly emotional fan reactions.  A new Nassau County law confiscating cars from drunk drivers sparked a debate about government overreach.  The Grammy Awards provided further fodder, with humorous commentary on nominees, including the surprising presence of Nashville Pussy in the Best Metal Performance category.

The next segment began with Grammy predictions, specifically focusing on Lauryn Hill, Celine Dion, and Brandy & Monica.  Anthony made several crude comments about Lauryn Hill's lips.  They expressed their dislike for the Backstreet Boys, predicting their quick demise, and mentioned Rosie O'Donnell hosting the ceremony and a "Best Polka Album" award.  Johnny Rotten's involvement in the pre-show led to speculation about a potential Backstreet Boys assault.  The conversation abruptly shifted to Kevin Costner's *Message in a Bottle*, with Opie spoiling the ending, much to the potential chagrin of listeners.  This led to angry and amused listener calls, prompting the hosts to consider a recurring segment of spoiling movies for their audience.  The show concluded with plans for their evening at Caroline's to see Stephen Lynch, alongside continued concerns about their website's server capacity, overwhelmed by traffic from the aforementioned "Tara pictures" and anticipated footage from their Valentine's Day party.
