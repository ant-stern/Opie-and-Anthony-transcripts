SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with comedian Bill Burr filling in for Jim Norton.  A significant portion was dedicated to Anthony's contentious divorce, detailing his ex-wife's seemingly unreasonable demands, particularly concerning household chores, and her later claims of contributing to his success.  Anecdotes about their life together, from their early struggling days in radio to the present, were shared.  The hosts then recounted their experiences at Mardi Gras in New Orleans, describing it as frightening and detailing incidents of public indecency and violence.  They reminisced about their excessive drinking and partying during the show's peak, mentioning shot girls, a Jägermeister machine, and the significant control they wielded due to high ratings.  Their attendance at a Rolling Stones concert the previous night was discussed, including a VIP party for XM Satellite Radio salespeople, and a comparison of Mick Jagger and James Hetfield’s stage presence.  A Stephen Lynch song was played, and "The War of the Kumias" segment was mentioned.

The show continued with a detailed account of the Rolling Stones concert, including the lengthy intermission between Alanis Morissette (who played to a sparse crowd) and the Rolling Stones, and observations about the band members' performances and personalities, particularly Ron Wood's family life.  The massive stage production was also described.  This transitioned into a heated argument regarding Eric Nagel, a show employee, winning $100,000 in a Daily News scratch-off lottery. The focus shifted to the hosts and staff allegedly abandoning Eric in the Meadowlands parking lot after the concert.  Accusations of negligence and intentional ditching were exchanged, with Eric detailing his struggles to get home, including encounters with "Stalker Patty."  Listener calls mostly sided with the hosts, leaving the argument unresolved.

Further conflict arose between Opie and Eric, revolving around Eric's perceived ingratitude for Opie's role in his career and a dispute over the legitimacy of Eric’s winning lottery ticket, which seemingly came from station promotional materials.  The argument escalated to threats of legal action, ultimately settling on a symbolic lunch.  Calls from Manny and Travis offered conflicting accounts of the ticket incident. Stalker Patty called in, describing her gambling habits and surprisingly uneventful encounters with Eric.  The show then arranged a date between Stalker Patty and Big A, a taxi driver, with a staff member as chaperone, planning to document it at Paisano's during the San Gennaro Feast. Opie concluded this segment with a story about being scammed at a similar game at the San Gennaro Feast.

A Paisano's restaurant promotion followed, featuring Dane Cook as a guest in the studio.  A phone call with Jim Norton, detailing his disastrous trip to Los Angeles (roach-infested apartment, unpleasant encounter with a woman) and anxieties about his flight on a financially troubled Northwest Airlines, was included.  Dane Cook and Bill Burr shared humorous stand-up experiences, recounting disastrous gigs and dealing with difficult audiences. Cook described a surreal MTV Video Music Awards appearance. Listener-submitted remixes of Bob Kelly’s knee injury audio became a recurring comedic element.  An irate listener called in, engaging in a heated exchange with the hosts.

A hostile phone call with a listener, "Dragon Wagon," featuring vulgar insults and crude remarks, was followed by a studio segment with Bill Burr promoting his HBO special. Dane Cook discussed his Madison Square Garden show and shared more stories of difficult gigs, often using offensive language and racial slurs in his retellings. Bob Kelly called in with stories about his gigs, including one involving defecation (either on a tour bus or in a wastebasket, depending on the account).  The hosts played a clip of Martha Stewart rapping with P. Diddy, commenting on its awkwardness using offensive language, and concluded with a brief discussion of Dane Cook's tour dates.
