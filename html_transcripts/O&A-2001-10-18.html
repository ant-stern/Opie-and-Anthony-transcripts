SHOW SUMMARY:

The October 18, 2001, Opie and Anthony Show was a raucous recap of their Philadelphia roadshow the previous day, heavily punctuated by listener calls,  audio clips from the event, and the hosts' characteristically crude humor. The broadcast opened with Opie and Anthony joking about their hangovers from the trip, which involved copious amounts of alcohol at a strip club (Cheerleaders) where Jim Norton acquired body glitter from lap dances, Jeff from Foundry Music passed out, and Jeff later vomited in a taxi. Opie recounted an encounter with a woman resembling Chyna.  The hosts contrasted the New York and Philadelphia audiences, highlighting the Philly women's enthusiastic participation, including frequent "wowing" (flashing) and beer-throwing.

The roadshow itself involved a bikini contest, a "couch dance" area offering more intimate encounters, and the repeated beer-bombing of "Stalker Patty," dressed as Super Lady, during her runway walk.  Numerous callers corroborated these accounts, adding details like the lead singer of Octane getting hit with beer and further descriptions of Jeff's extreme intoxication. One caller recounted having sex in a bathroom with someone claiming to be from WYSP, only to be interrupted by bouncers.  Brother Joe, who opened the show dressed as Osama bin Laden, was pelted with beers, Sweet'n Low, and paintballs fired by Anthony.  Photos and videos from the event were repeatedly advertised as available on opieandanthony.com and foundrymusic.com, showing Brother Joe's welts and Anthony's paintball gun.

The show included a call with Ozzy Osbourne, who was en route to an in-store appearance via helicopter and exhibited slurred speech.  The hosts and Norton discussed Ozzy's new album.  Audio clips from the bus ride back featured two intoxicated women, referred to as "retarded Laverne and Shirley," repeating "yin-yang party" and engaging in nonsensical conversation.  Other audio snippets included a foul-mouthed Tom Brokaw impersonation.  Further audio from the bus captured intoxicated conversations between Lady Diane Marion and Patty, detailing their on-stage actions (including exposing breasts and buttocks),  alcohol consumption (Lady Di claiming 10-12 drinks),  and Lady Di's attraction to "Stinky," along with Patty threatening to cut a man's genitals.  The hosts lamented segments of the road show audio being censored ("dumped") by the station, specifically referencing jokes about tattoos and gum.

Comedian Stephen Lynch, who performed at the road show, called in, mentioning a distracting floor-mopper and an impromptu nude performance by "Hot Liz."  The hosts announced upcoming shows in Boston (a "Grave Rave" Halloween party) and Washington D.C., acknowledging the latter's apparent dislike of the show.  They also proposed a "boob-a-lantern" contest.  Other segments included a foul-mouthed Tom Brokaw impersonation reacting to the anthrax attacks, and promotional segments for WWF Smackdown and Restaurant Week in New York City.

The show also included a prank call segment from their "Art to Menor World" CD, a discussion about a Spinal Tap concert where the band allegedly behaved rudely and blew off the show's promotional efforts, despite Anthony's "entourage" being denied backstage access.  This was followed by a crude commentary on a fictionalized VH1 Def Leppard "Behind the Music" style movie involving dismemberment and drug use.  The conversation included references to Penn Jillette, Stephen Van Zandt, and various *Saturday Night Live* cast members, and an encounter between a man from *Will & Grace* (described as the gay character trying to appear heterosexual) and another attendee involving homophobic slurs. Jim Brewer was mentioned as a scheduled guest but didn't appear.  The broadcast concluded with an angry caller failing to respond when his name was called, leading to Opie's frustrated outburst.
