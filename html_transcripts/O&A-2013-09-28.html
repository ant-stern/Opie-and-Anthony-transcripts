The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast covered a wide range of topics, featuring numerous celebrity guests and callers.  The show opened with Sam Roberts hosting a "Worst of the Week Countdown,"  during which a recurring listener, Bobo, repeatedly called in, oblivious to the show's context, creating humorous exchanges with the hosts and comedian Colin Quinn.  In-studio guests during this segment included James Caan, Michael Shannon, Stephen Merchant, and Jim Florentine.

A substantial portion of the broadcast focused on Kanye West's feud with Jimmy Kimmel, sparked by Kimmel's parody of a Kanye West interview.  The hosts played clips of Kanye's angry tweets and interview responses, highlighting his aggressive and narcissistic behavior. They also played a Dennis Falcone bit parodying Dickie Goodman, using contemporary hip-hop artists and frequent use of the n-word, and discussed Kimmel's response, which involved a prank phone call from a supposedly enraged Kanye demanding an apology.  The hosts predicted Kimmel would win the public relations battle.

The show then shifted to Jim Norton's unsuccessful quest for a photo with Metallica after a SiriusXM concert.  Norton, despite attending a meet-and-greet, missed his chance with James Hetfield, leading to frantic attempts to secure a photo, even interrupting a mid-interview with in-studio guest Tony Danza. The hosts detailed the ensuing chaos and ultimately unsuccessful efforts to gather the full band for a picture.  Tony Danza and Michael Shannon were both in-studio guests during this segment.

An extended interview with Michael Shannon followed.
