SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opens with a discussion of Barack Obama's 2008 presidential election victory.  Opie expresses his dissatisfaction with the results, revealing he bought an assault rifle in anticipation of stricter gun control under an Obama administration and plans to post videos of his time at the gun range. Anthony appears more apathetic.  They dissect various reactions to the election, including international responses and misremembered comments attributed to Jesse Jackson (initially thought to be directed at Osama bin Laden).  The hosts lampoon the news media's emotional, rather than analytical, coverage of the election.  A significant portion of the show centers on a running gag about Opie owing Anthony $100 from an election bet. Anthony, refusing to pay, satirizes Obama's policies by claiming he's redistributing the wealth.  This leads to a humorous power struggle where Anthony, leveraging his perceived authority, avoids repaying the debt.  A studio EMT is present and checks Anthony's blood pressure during this exchange. No other guests besides the EMT are mentioned.
