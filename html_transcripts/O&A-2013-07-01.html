SHOW SUMMARY:

This Opie and Anthony Show broadcast, hosted by Sam Roberts in the absence of Opie and Anthony, was a "worst of 2013 so far" compilation.  The show began with a humorous recounting of Opie's three-year-old son's sugar-fueled rampage, which included destroying a 65-inch TV with a T-ball and taking an unsupervised elevator ride across multiple floors of their 50-story building.

This was followed by a previously aired interview with James Lipton. Lipton discussed his career at *Inside the Actor's Studio*, detailing his interview techniques (avoiding pre-interviews for spontaneity) and sharing anecdotes from his celebrity interviews.  A particularly shocking revelation was Lipton's admission to working as a male escort ("mech") in Paris during the 1950s, contextualizing it within the social norms of the era. He also recounted emotionally charged interviews with Bradley Cooper and Jack Lemmon, notably Lemmon's candid confession of alcoholism.  The conversation touched on transatlantic differences in attitudes toward sex and the impact of the Stanislavski system on American acting.

The show then shifted to a discussion of acting techniques, focusing on the challenges actors face, particularly with hand placement, using Humphrey Bogart as an example.  This transitioned into an extensive analysis of various interviews with Charles Ramsey, the Cleveland man who helped rescue three missing women.  The hosts played several interviews, highlighting inconsistencies in his statements, his colorful language ("bitches," "motherfucker"), and his frequent mention of McDonald's. They speculated about his future, predicting both fame and potential legal trouble, and compared his case to the Jaycee Dugard kidnapping, criticizing law enforcement's shortcomings in both instances.  Ramsey's unfiltered 911 call was played, alongside discussion of the 911 operator's unprofessionalism and Ramsey's possibly evolving story.

The show's engineer, Mars, was then interviewed. He revealed details about his childhood physical and emotional abuse at the hands of his mother, including incidents involving a hot spatula and being trapped under a stereo system.  He detailed ongoing challenges maintaining a relationship with his mother, despite her self-absorbed behavior.  The hosts expressed empathy and support.  Mars further expanded on his abuse, recounting numerous instances of physical assault by his mother, with his father present but uninvolved. He described his mother's bizarre habits, including urinating in bedpans.  A caller, Ken, shared a similar story of parental neglect and substance abuse, emphasizing his decision to cut ties with his parents to prevent repeating the pattern. Mars discussed his adult life, his strained relationship with his mother, and his wife's encouragement to maintain contact.  He also recounted his own childhood mischief and stealing, and incidents of bullying at school.

Mars's discussion of abuse continued, triggered by a conversation about bathroom convenience.  He detailed further instances of abuse (extension cord beatings), the conversation branching into types of corporal punishment and humorous, yet dark, tangents involving hot spatulas.  Troy Kwonk was briefly mentioned. The conversation touched upon Mars's current relationship with his mother, his reluctance to cut ties, and his feelings of unlovability.  Other contributors shared their experiences with parental abuse and discipline.  Mars discussed how his upbringing affected his parenting style.

Moses Gates, an urban explorer and author of "Hidden Cities," joined the show.  He recounted his exploits with Opie, including exploring under the West Side Highway. They discussed the dangers and excitement of urban exploration, and interactions with security and the public.

A later segment focused on Gates' book, "Hidden Cities."  Gates detailed his illegal entry into the Chrysler Building (reaching the 61st floor and sitting on an eagle statue), climbing to its top, exploring the Naglinka River in Moscow (nearly getting arrested), the Paris catacombs, Egyptian pyramids, and New York bridges and subways.  He discussed his methods, risks, and global network of urban explorers.  His arrest in France for climbing Notre Dame was mentioned.  The segment concluded with the hosts and Gates developing a comedic cop show concept featuring "The Goose," based on Gates' experiences, and a promotional announcement for the show's return after a vacation.
