SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast from WNEW began with the hosts' return to the station, immediately launching into commentary on the summer heat and pungent smells of New York City.  Opie shared a bizarre anecdote about witnessing a city worker unexpectedly turning a women's road race in Central Park into a wet T-shirt contest while he was rollerblading.  The conversation then shifted to comedian Dice, whose faded comedic career was subjected to their ribbing.  A news item about Amish people involved in cocaine trafficking followed, sparking humorous speculation on the arrest process.  Their experiences staying in New York hotels were discussed, along with a scathing critique of a poorly-received television segment, "The Magic Hour," which they renamed "The Tragic Hour."

Throughout the show, numerous listener faxes and phone calls were mentioned, including a welcoming fax from Vince Skelson.  They promoted "A Day in the Garden" music festival at Yasgur's Farm, listing some of the performers.  The hosts offered their opinions on the Bruce Willis and Demi Moore breakup.  Further topics included the absurdity of television traffic reports in the modern age, observations about New York City life (including people using eyelash curlers while driving), President Clinton's visit to China and Monica Lewinsky's presence in the city.  Opie expressed his dislike of female body hair.  They recounted their time in Boston, including an on-air prank that led to their departure from the station.  Celebrity sightings (Gene Simmons, Mark Gastineau, Tony Bennett) were also mentioned.

A significant portion of the show was dedicated to reading listener faxes, many of which simply said "butt plug." A prank call mimicking President Clinton, with a suggestive double entendre involving his interpreter's name as the punchline, was aired.  The hosts shared their experiences with WNEW staff, specifically mentioning Scott Muni, and even provided a traffic report.  Their conversation included a discussion of their previous experiences at WNEW, and they concluded the show with further promotion of the music festival and speculation on the current "summer song."
