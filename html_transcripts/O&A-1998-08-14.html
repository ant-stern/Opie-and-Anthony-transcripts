SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with the hosts boasting about their increasing popularity, evidenced by jammed phone lines.  They then launched into a satirical bit about disastrous Sonny and Cher audition attempts in Times Square, describing the participants with harsh, judgmental, and crude language.  The conversation shifted to newly discovered letters revealing Eleanor Roosevelt's lesbian relationship, which the hosts discussed using graphic sexual language and crude jokes, reading excerpts from the letters aloud. This segment was followed by a flurry of listener calls, ranging from positive feedback to furious, obscenity-laced attacks directed at Opie and Anthony. One particularly heated exchange with an angry female caller devolved into a mutual volley of vulgar insults, a clip of which was considered for use in a future promotional spot.

Next, the hosts played a recording of their commute, featuring Anthony's profanity-laced tirades at other drivers and their commentary on pedestrians. A contest winner then visited the studio to claim their Van Halen tickets. This visit involved a humorous, albeit potentially insensitive, segment where the hosts attempted (and failed) to use a purported x-ray camera on her, culminating in an accidental flash and their subsequent comments.  Al Sharpton then joined the show via phone to deliver a comedic traffic report riddled with racial slurs and politically charged commentary. The broadcast concluded with further discussion about the listener response, a repeat of a previous segment featuring a caller complaining about the hosts' use of the word "pussy," and a final discussion about Bill Clinton's impending testimony concerning the Monica Lewinsky scandal.
