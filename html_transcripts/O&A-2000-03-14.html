SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with Anthony detailing a three-hour ordeal involving Tel Aviv Car Service.  After a show, he waited excessively for a car, eventually engaging in a heated argument with the driver who ejected him.  A subsequent call to the manager, Barry, resulted in an apology and a promised driver suspension.  However, this led to Opie and Anthony encouraging listeners to flood the car service (777-7777) with prank calls, accompanied by highly offensive comments from Anthony regarding the driver's ethnicity.

Following this, the "What's Up" guys—Paul, Fred, and Scott from a Budweiser commercial—appeared in studio. They discussed their famous catchphrase, the commercial's creation, and the show's history of on-air drinking, culminating in a humorous reading of a memo prohibiting alcohol on the show.

The show then shifted to listener experiences at a WWF Raw is War event at the Meadowlands.  Numerous callers described the numerous listener-created signs, including "O&A," "Okay, Terrific," "Wow," "FBA," "Rusty Trombone," "Whale Eye," "Leather Cheerio," and "We put the M in stupid."  Callers recounted seeing Psycho Mark and Spaz at the event, engaging in antics and interacting with the crowd.  The discussion included details about WWF performers like Vince McMahon, The Rock, Mae Young, and Moolah, their performances, and audience reactions. One caller recounted nearly causing a car accident due to a startlingly realistic gunshot sound effect during the broadcast.  Listeners also reported the vandalism of a rival radio station's van.  The hosts discussed a potential future WWF appearance, possibly involving a ring invasion.  A listener who was suspended from his job for singing an offensive song also called in.  The segment included further discussion of offensive songs and the show’s bulletin board, with additional input from a group of Hofstra University students who attended the event.  Psycho Mark also made an in-studio appearance.  Throughout the segments, vulgar and sexually suggestive banter occurred between the hosts and callers.
