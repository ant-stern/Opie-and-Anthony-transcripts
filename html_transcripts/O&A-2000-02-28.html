SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast on February 28, 2000, was a whirlwind of topics, ranging from serious discussions to outrageously vulgar humor and celebrity appearances.  The show opened with intense anger from Opie and Anthony regarding the acquittal of the police officers in the Amadou Diallo shooting and the subsequent protests. They voiced strong opinions about the protests and the perceived unfair treatment of police officers, with several callers, including some police officers, sharing their experiences and reinforcing the hosts’ views.  The conversation frequently touched on race relations and perceived bias within the legal system.

This anger abruptly shifted when WWE wrestler Ivory surprised them in studio with the news that they, along with a guest each, had won an all-expenses-paid trip to WrestleMania 2000 in Anaheim. The excitement over the trip dominated the conversation for a significant portion, with discussions about Ivory’s career, behind-the-scenes stories from the WWE, and interactions with other wrestlers like Mae Young and Chyna.  This  announcement also marked a turning point from the heated discussion of the Diallo case and the protests. A significant portion of the show was dedicated to a phone interview with Ivory, again focusing on her career in wrestling and featuring sexually suggestive and crude humor both from the hosts and callers.  The hosts and Ivory also discussed their recent feud and subsequent reconciliation with the WWF, explaining that the WrestleMania trip was a peace offering.  This reconciliation led to significant listener backlash, with numerous calls debating whether the hosts had "sold out," a debate that occupied a considerable portion of the airtime.

Simultaneously,  a recurring bit involved Spaz, a show employee, who was sent outside to wear a "I Am Spaz" shirt and endure being yelled at by listeners for a chance to win wrestling tickets.  This street stunt, documented in real time via phone calls, contributed to the show's comedic content, along with a later segment where Spaz attempted to pick up women.  The hosts also discussed potential morning show shifts (which they rejected), a possible show in Boston, and the show's online message board.  Music from Creed and Ozzy Osbourne played throughout.

The show then moved to a discussion of Darryl Strawberry's suspension from Major League Baseball due to a failed drug test. This discussion ranged from sympathetic to highly critical views on Strawberry's addiction, with numerous jokes and opinions about his drug use and appearance. The conversation also touched upon perceived double standards in how athletes and average citizens are treated regarding drug offenses.  This segment also included a comedic visit from Stalker Patty, and a humorous interaction via cell phone through a locked studio door, mimicking a prison visit.

The show continued with discussions about the HBO show "If These Walls Could Talk," and the explicit content of "Real Sex," featuring graphic descriptions of various scenes from the show.
