SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with the hosts recounting their hangover from a party with Sammy Hagar the previous night, which continued at the Hard Rock Cafe.  They then read a critical letter from Kenneth Walker of Jamaica condemning their parody song mocking Reverend Al Sharpton, calling it disrespectful to minorities and biased against Sharpton compared to their treatment of Mayor Giuliani.  Opie and Anthony defended the song, arguing its accuracy and highlighting their frequent criticism of Giuliani, while criticizing Sharpton's lack of response to a local crime.

The show then hosted a disastrous interview with "The Greaseman," who promoted a new line of crayons with offensively named colors like "Chink Yellow," "Homo Pink," "Polish Purple," and "Wetback Brown." This led to his immediate removal from the studio by CBS executives. The hosts also discussed their positive experience at Sammy Hagar's show and a previous caller's comments about Hagar's contributions to Van Halen. A moment of silence was observed for another previous caller.

The conversation shifted to Joe Torre's prostate cancer diagnosis and the high number of Yankees who have been ill, prompting jokes about the stadium being built on nuclear waste.  They then played an audio clip of a sleepy Ted Williams being interviewed by Tom Brokaw.  The show received a call from someone claiming to be Babe Ruth calling from hell, filled with vulgar and offensive language, rambling anecdotes, and jokes.  They discussed car insurance costs, with Anthony complaining about a significant increase due to an old speeding ticket.  The show concluded with discussions of newly released tapes containing Nixon's racist remarks, more jokes about the Babe Ruth call, Barbie's 40th birthday, childhood memories and toys, and a listener-submitted video of Ken and Barbie dolls.

A subsequent segment began with a discussion of George Steinbrenner's emotional displays, contrasting his current tearfulness with his past combative nature. They reminisced about his feuds, particularly with Billy Martin, before transitioning to discussions of Joe Torre and Babe Ruth.  An audio clip of a speech Ruth gave to Little Leaguers shortly before his death was played.  This was followed by a mention of a (presumably prank) phone call from Babe Ruth from "hell," described as containing "salty" language and details about his womanizing, drinking, and Joe DiMaggio's afterlife.

The segment's latter half focused on a listener's email accusing Opie of dressing up his cats and photographing them. Opie vehemently denied this, leading to humorous banter and a discussion of the word "cunk," a word they had previously invented and used on a different show. They recounted the resulting controversy, including angry letters and calls, playfully repeating the word and emphasizing its absence from the dictionary. A caller used a similar-sounding word, causing a frantic, humorous moment as the hosts tried to avoid saying a censored word before their commercial break. The show ended with promotions for Stephen Lynch's performances at Caroline's.
