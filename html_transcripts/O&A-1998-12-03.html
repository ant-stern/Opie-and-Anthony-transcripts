SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show opened with a discussion about Blue Oyster Cult's original name, "Soft White Underbelly," and the hosts' past concert experiences.  This segued into a conversation about the controversy surrounding the children's book "Nappy Hair" and the backlash faced by a white Brooklyn teacher for reading it to her class. Opie and Anthony announced their plan to have celebrities read the book, with William Shatner already confirmed.

The show then moved on to a listener-participation segment focused on generating creative insults for Rudy Giuliani, specifically targeting his head.  Listeners were encouraged to submit entries for a countdown of the "top 1,027 names for Mayor Giuliani's head," with examples like "Quisp," "Beluga Whale," and "Simon Barsinister" being offered as inspiration.

Next, the hosts discussed their observations of overly solicitous doormen at a Citibank ATM who acted as de facto valets and expected tips. This sparked a broader conversation about various street people and their methods of panhandling.

A major portion of the show was dedicated to playing a previously recorded phone call.  Three years prior, Opie had mistakenly informed a woman who called him with a wrong number that her friend Bill had died.  Before playing the recording, the hosts warned sensitive listeners to switch stations.  Anthony threatened to retaliate by airing a recording of his own prank call to a funeral home, a threat he carried over to the closing segment. The broadcast concluded with a promotion for an upcoming Aerosmith giveaway and further teasing of Anthony's prank call for a future show.
