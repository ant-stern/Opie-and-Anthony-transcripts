SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with a discussion about comedian Artie Lange's experiences with the Ron and Paul show on Miami's Big 106, a classic rock station.  This led to a broader conversation about comedian Aerie Spears' disastrous interview on the same show.  Spears, from *MadTV*, had apparently been criticized on-air after leaving the studio, prompting his return to confront the hosts.  This set the stage for a discussion about the often-harsh treatment of comedians by radio hosts.

A significant portion of the segment, however, was unexpectedly dominated by Opie's ongoing saga of a piece of studio equipment repeatedly falling on his foot.  This resulted in much laughter and frustrated commentary from Opie, with the other hosts and guest comedian Bob Kelly joining in the ribbing.  Bob Kelly's presence in the studio briefly shifted the conversation to his hybrid car and his opinions on California.  The hosts also mentioned other comedians, including the one they described as "psychotic," Dan Natterman, and Dan Cook, who also had a negative radio experience in Miami.  A listener, Ryan, called in to express a mixture of sympathy and mocking disbelief regarding Opie's foot injury.

A substantial portion of the segment was dedicated to playing and reacting to audio of Aerie Spears' explosive confrontation with the Miami radio hosts. This audio prompted further discussion about the perceived blandness and homogenization of many commercial radio personalities.  The hosts continued to dissect Spears' performance, employing the word "diddly" to describe it, along with various other off-color jokes and comments, ultimately ending the segment with lingering commentary on the confrontation.
