SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a discussion of the hosts' weekend activities, focusing heavily on their video game experiences and subsequent movie-going misadventures. Opie detailed his frustrating struggle with a particularly challenging section in Tomb Raider 3, involving backflips and a cobra, hilariously suggesting he needed a 12-year-old's assistance.  He also complained about the resulting numb thumbs.  Anthony described his immersive, almost delirious, experience playing Half-Life multiplayer for an extended period.

This led to a discussion about the "chick flicks" they were forced to endure by their girlfriends as penance for their gaming marathons. Opie specifically criticized "You've Got Mail," mocking its AOL product placement and unrealistic portrayal of New York City.  Other films deemed excruciatingly boring included "Bridges of Madison County," "Lethal Weapon 4" (criticized for its portrayal of Chinese villains), and later in the show, "Beaches," "Steel Magnolias," and "The Horse Whisperer."  Listener calls were interspersed, with many sharing their own experiences of being dragged to unwanted movies, and one caller even sharing their experience watching "Ford Fairlane," prompting an angry defense of the film by a guest, "Dice."

The show incorporated a Godfather trivia contest with a Super Bowl party at the winner's home, featuring a large-screen TV, as the prize.  The hosts also reminisced about classic arcade games like *Asteroids*, *Joust*, *Defender*, *Pac-Man*, and *Centipede*, contrasting them with modern gaming technology.  The discussion further touched upon excessive movie previews in modern theaters.

A significant portion of the show involved personal anecdotes. Opie's lawyer, Paul, read a list of embarrassing stories about Opie, including his questionable movie tastes, a transvestite encounter in the studio, and his childhood crush on Mrs. Roper.  Anthony's childhood crush on Tatum O'Neal was also revealed, sparking a conversation about the hosts' own childhood crushes, with Opie sharing that his first wet dream involved Nadia Comaneci.  The hosts also greeted police officers and the audience of a previous radio show host, Rocky Allen, and explained their decision to stay in afternoon drive time instead of competing with Howard Stern in the morning.

The broadcast also included a mention of a false report of Joe DiMaggio's death on NBC news and the revelation that one of the Village People was homeless. The show concluded with final jokes and promotions, seamlessly integrating listener feedback and humorous anecdotes throughout the entire broadcast.  Besides the hosts, the only in-studio guests mentioned were "Dice" and the show's lawyer, Paul, along with radio show staff member, Rick.
