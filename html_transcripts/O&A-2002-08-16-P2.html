SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast was significantly disrupted by legal restrictions, primarily stemming from a listener contest resulting in arrests.  The hosts, Opie and Anthony, spent a large portion of the show apologizing profusely ("My bad!") and filling airtime with nonsensical outbursts and profanity, repeatedly hampered by their lawyers' instructions preventing discussion of the contest.  This led to extensive use of filler, including random soundbites and tangential topics.  Jim Norton was present in the studio throughout much of the broadcast.

A significant portion focused on the disastrous stand-up performance of Billy Staples at a Jim Norton show.  The hosts played an audio recording of the performance, detailing Staples' heckling and subsequent distress, with a caller sharing their firsthand account.  This Staples' segment was revisited throughout the show, with a planned segment repeatedly delayed and snippets played across multiple sections.  Ron from the Ron and Fez show offered commentary on Staples' performance in one segment.  A scathing review of Staples' performance was also played and discussed.

The hosts' anxieties and internal conflicts were also apparent.  They discussed their "paranoia" surrounding certain situations, notably the arrest of former radio competitor Mark Parenteau on charges of sexual assault of a minor, recounting anecdotes and jokes about him.  Anthony launched into an offensive rant about children, later attempting to clarify and distance himself, leading to a satirizing bit by "Martini Steve."  Brian Regan was a guest in the studio during multiple segments, offering comedic relief and sharing his experiences with bombing on stage, particularly in contrast to Staples' panicked response.

Other topics included the ongoing baseball strike, eliciting cynical commentary from the hosts, and a discussion about a "no money excuse pool" among the hosts.   They also commented on a Tawny Stone pornography banner ad on their website and played listener-submitted "F.U." messages containing extremely offensive language.  A planned appearance by Anthony Michael Hall was cancelled due to illness.

The show also inexplicably centered around peanut butter and jelly sandwiches, with the hosts joking about the absurdity of this topic being off-limits due to perceived legal risks.  They speculated on pre-mixed peanut butter and jelly jars, creating bizarre tangents.  The “PBJ thing” was referenced frequently throughout the broadcast and even surfaced in a local news segment.

The broadcast included numerous advertisements for a wide variety of products and services, including the Ohio State Fair, Monroe Muffler, Columbus State Community College, Sprint Solutions, Kroger, Coors Original, Guitar Center, Mitsubishi Motors, SBC Ameritech, Labatt Blue, Travelocity, Jack Daniels hard cola, a gentleman's club, car dealerships, utilities, a newspaper, a malt beverage,  *Collateral Damage*, Cedar Point, *The Other Paper*, Dennis Pontiac, McDonald's, Speeds European Indoor Karting,  and DirecTV, among others.

Technical difficulties, specifically Opie's malfunctioning microphone requiring an emergency repair by Pete Johnson (who was noted to be retiring), and a past incident involving a couple having sex in the studio resulting in carpet replacement were also mentioned. Pat Croce called in to discuss his new extreme sport, "Slam Ball," providing a description and showcasing a video of the game.  A planned UK interview and other segments were cancelled at 2:40 PM due to unspecified restrictions, leading to further frustration and improvisation.  Listener calls filled part of the remaining time, with callers sharing their daily experiences.  The show concluded with a song and continued references to the unresolved situation and ongoing restrictions.
