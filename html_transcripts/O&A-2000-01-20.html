SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a segment dedicated to listener calls detailing harrowing divorce experiences. Callers shared intensely personal and often angry stories.  Nick recounted his wife's post-$12,000-vacation divorce filing, leaving him with three jobs and his '74 Camaro. Florence described her ex-husband's car sabotage. Frank detailed his ex-wife's Xanax abuse and the resulting chaotic behavior that, ironically, worked to his advantage during the divorce. Carl shared his story of his wife's affair and the subsequent financial fallout.

The show then took a dramatic turn with a call from Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson.  The Rock apologized for abruptly ending a previous appearance due to the presence of a guest who had posted racist comments online.  Opie and Anthony initially refused to let him on the air due to a prior incident where the WWF (and The Rock) had denied them entry to a party despite having an RSVP.  A lengthy, heated, and humorous exchange followed, referencing the hosts' "award-winning" status.  The Rock's apology was eventually accepted, though the hosts maintained their criticism of the WWF's actions. This incident, and Opie's graphic and vulgar song about his own divorce (which was played on air), spurred numerous listener calls throughout the show.  Two large police officers also visited the studio for security reasons during this segment.

The show continued with more caller interactions, including a sexually suggestive and vulgar exchange with a female caller, Bridget, who had previously been a participant in a segment designed to make her cry.  Bridget sent pictures of herself, prompting discussion of their suitability for the unofficial O&A website's "Girls of the O&A Show" section. Steve, from the unofficial website, was present in the studio and briefly participated in viewing the photos. Callers then shared their takeaways from the show, with comments ranging from humorous observations on professional wrestling (WWE vs. WCW) to cynical remarks about marriage. One caller mentioned being a Stone Cold Steve Austin look-alike and his plans to attend the Royal Rumble. The hosts also discussed their recent "Best Afternoon Radio Show" win at the New York Metro Air Awards and the negative reactions they received from some in the radio industry. The show concluded with the hosts' usual closing remarks and a promotion of "FU Friday."
