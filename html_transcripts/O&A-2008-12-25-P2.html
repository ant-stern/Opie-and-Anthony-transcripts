SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with Dennis Leary's comedic song about a sexually suggestive interpretation of the nativity story.  This transitioned into the second annual "Homeless Shopping Spree," a live broadcast from a bus carrying ten homeless individuals, each given $100, to Roosevelt Field Mall.  The bus, equipped with bathrooms and pornography playing on screens, was a scene of chaos from the start.  Passengers, nicknamed "Sperm," "Tippy Tom," "Rock and Roll Tommy," and "Blue," among others, engaged in fights, intoxication, urination, and even defecation.  Staff members Ben, Rick, Psycho Mark, and Jim Norton, along with the hosts, attempted to manage the increasingly absurd situation, while providing commentary and interviewing callers.  Jim Norton repeatedly expressed his need to use the bathroom. Tippi Tom repeatedly fell asleep, scratched himself, and threatened violence; Rock and Roll Tommy threatened self-harm and later had an "alcoholic seizure"; and Blue displayed erratic and aggressive behavior.  Ziggy believed he was on an airplane.  The hosts, facing escalating tension and considering the potential consequences of giving the passengers beer, ultimately decided to do so, despite Keith's warnings of a potential riot.  Callers reported a large following of cars and rumors of police involvement at the mall.

The arrival at Roosevelt Field Mall was met with significant commotion, a large crowd, and a heavy police presence.  While largely celebratory, the police were needed to manage the crowd and the increasingly erratic behavior of some participants.  Several stores, including Victoria's Secret and Armani, initially refused entry.  The shopping spree itself was chaotic, with one individual jumping into a bathtub display.  Participants bought various items, including clothes, a boombox, and a radio.  The event concluded with the group going to a bar, resulting in further intoxication among some participants.  Throughout this segment, the hosts and guests used extensive profanity.

The aftermath of the shopping spree featured more phone calls from Psycho Mark, Rick, Ben, and Steve detailing intoxicated participants, enthusiastic crowds, and incidents such as Psycho Mark losing his teeth and Andre nearly exposing himself.  A caller criticized the show's lack of originality.  This transitioned into a recording of Frankie Blue, the program director of their former station WNEW, drunkenly broadcasting a radio show after an apparent argument with a female DJ, repeatedly mispronouncing the station's call letters. The hosts and callers speculated on his likely firing and discussed WNEW's struggles since Opie and Anthony's departure.  The "Homeless for the Holidays" CD was promoted.

The broadcast continued with a discussion of Frankie Blue's firing, which involved yelling and potentially manhandling a female co-host; an audio clip was played.  The hosts used homophobic slurs to describe corporate radio executives.  This led to a promotion for a RuPaul-involved contest with a $50,000 prize.  An interview with the cast of *A Christmas Story* (Zach Ward, Ian Petrella, and Scott Schwartz) followed, covering their experiences filming the movie and explicit details about Scott Schwartz's adult film career.  The conversation then shifted to Christmas, featuring jokes about gift-giving, technology, and personal experiences, interspersed with listener calls.  "Golden Buns" repeatedly exposed his buttocks on air.

The show continued with a highly inappropriate and sexually suggestive discussion about Robert Reed, using homophobic slurs and making crude jokes. This segued into a profane, sexually suggestive Christmas skit featuring Santa Claus.  A listener called, expressing anger about the Santa Claus jokes made in the presence of his three-year-old son.  The hosts spoke to the child, telling him "there's no Santa Claus."  The broadcast concluded with a Christmas carol performance by Ziggy and repeated website addresses.
