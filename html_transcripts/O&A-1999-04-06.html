SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast covered a wide range of topics,  beginning with observations about women shedding winter clothing and transitioning into a discussion of listener-made signs at a WWE Raw event. These signs included one suggesting a phone number for gay sex and another referencing “Opie and Anthony plus Vince McMahon equal three wise men, Ted Turner is a butt plug.”  The hosts heavily promoted their website's newly uploaded content, which included sexually explicit audio clips.  Several listener calls and emails fueled the discussion, particularly concerning their "Whip Him Out Wednesday" segment encouraging the display of "WOW" signs for sightings of scantily clad women.  One caller recounted an early "WOW" sighting, and another described a woman in a card store disrobing after seeing his sign.

The show featured numerous audio clips, including an angry voicemail from a disgruntled listener insulting the hosts and their mothers, and a "Hi, Mom" call where a son confessed to being fired for masturbating at work. Anthony and his brother’s song was also played.  The hosts interspersed listener feedback, both positive and negative, with advertisements for sponsors: MindSpring internet service, Men’s Hair Now hairpieces, and Stacker 2 diet pills.  A concert announcement for Hole at Roseland, facilitated by Ben, described as “Ben the Fag,” a studio guest, was included.  A phone call with Larry Flynt added to the show’s eclectic mix, with Flynt recounting having seen Courtney Love naked.

Later in the broadcast, Opie and Anthony teased the announcement of their upcoming broadcast location for the following day, and requested physical submissions for a design contest instead of digital ones.  A caller accused them of bashing Howard Stern, prompting a defense from the hosts, who clarified that discussion didn’t equate to bashing and that Stern was capable of defending himself.  They jokingly compared Stern's wealth to that of a listener with a significantly lower income.  The hosts further generated excitement for their upcoming "WOW" event, sharing anecdotes about listener involvement, including truck drivers' plans to incorporate "WOW" into their work.  A caller, an audio engineer, discussed his wife's potential participation in the "WOW" event, sparking humorous conversation about her breast size and the hosts' preferences.  Opie concluded the segment by taking credit for many of the show's controversial ideas.
