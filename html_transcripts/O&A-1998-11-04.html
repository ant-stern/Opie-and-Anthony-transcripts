SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a furious debate about WNEW's airing of a "classic rock" music special. Opie and Anthony, vehemently opposed to the playlist, argued it was outdated and alienated their younger audience, harming the station's ratings.  They engaged in a heated exchange with Carol Miller, a station employee brought in to defend the special's selection.  The argument escalated, with Opie and Anthony using insults and accusing other station staff of cowardice.  Carol, unmoved, even brought enemas into the studio as a symbolic gesture of defiance. The discussion involved numerous references to specific artists like Marshall Crenshaw, Rod Stewart, Metallica, The Stray Cats, Frank Zappa, and John Mellencamp, interspersed with personal anecdotes from Opie and Anthony and listener calls both supporting and opposing their stance.  Station manager Scott Herman arrived to observe the unfolding conflict.  The segment included discussion of the station's past ratings and the tension between its history and its need to adapt to contemporary tastes.  Despite their objections, the show continued playing the classic rock special, periodically interrupting it with listener polls.

Later in the broadcast, a listener phoned in, angrily criticizing the show's content and referencing internal station conflicts, specifically mentioning the "Carol Miller incident." Opie and Anthony dismissed the caller, with one host telling him to "get the hell off" the phone. The conversation grew heated, resulting in the use of a delay due to profanity.  Following this contentious call, the hosts briefly acknowledged having supporters, with a caller expressing appreciation for the show, including their 14-year-old daughter.  The show concluded with the hosts repeatedly promising to return the next day to continue their "Rock of New York" segment.
