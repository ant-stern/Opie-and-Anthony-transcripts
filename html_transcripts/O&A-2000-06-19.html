SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show began with Anthony unveiling a new haircut, ending a seven-year cap-wearing streak.  This prompted considerable teasing from Opie and Rick, who compared his new 'do to various celebrities (David Cassidy, Ricky Martin, David Hasselhoff, and a Cocker Spaniel), and commented on the $130 price tag.  The discussion meandered through Rick's hair products, a failed Ben Stein interview attempt, and plans to post photos of Anthony's hair on the website.  Listeners called in to offer their opinions on the new look.

The show then shifted to a review of Friday's "55-gallon drum challenge," using photos and audio clips.  The hosts and callers critiqued the embarrassing weekend WNEW programming, particularly a sweeper using poor quality music and announcing the show as "Powered by Opie and Anthony."  They heavily criticized the weekend DJs' playlist choices (mentioning Billy Squier) and suggested airing "best of" weekday segments.

Next came a discussion of the destruction of numerous Bruce Springsteen CDs, a reaction to Springsteen's song "American Skin (41 Shots)."  The hosts debated their criticism of Springsteen and their right to respond to his music.  The show concluded with a bizarre story about a woman found in the hosts' office, apparently invited by a coworker, Earl, leading to accusations against Earl for unauthorized access, and a heated exchange about his possession of a master key.

A later segment focused intensely on Earl's unauthorized presence in the hosts' office with a woman.  Earl's explanations were challenged, revealing his access to a master key through the chief engineer, and culminating in a heated discussion, with potential implications of Earl leaving the show.  This transitioned back to a recap of the "55-gallon drum challenge," with calls from participants and Tabitha's husband, John, who expressed extreme anger over his wife's treatment. John detailed the substances (including unspecified disgusting materials) and Tabitha's distress, including nightmares and excessive showering. Wendy, another participant, also called, describing her allergic reactions and lingering bad smells.  Captain Anthony, a caller, recounted witnessing John's intense negative reactions.  Photos and videos of the event were mentioned as being available online.  The hosts also made tasteless jokes about Don Imus's horse-riding accident (five broken ribs, a collapsed lung, a broken collarbone, and a separated shoulder), and the Imus/Curtis Schwealer ratings battle.  Steve from foundrymusic.com was in the studio during this segment.

Another segment featured Wendy recounting her "55-gallon drum challenge" experience, including the smell, bugs, and overall unpleasantness,  with plans for an even "sicker" sequel.  The discussion then moved to disruptive moviegoers, particularly at a *Shaft* showing, with callers sharing anecdotes often involving racially charged humor. A final segment focused on the aftermath of the "55-gallon drum challenge," featuring calls from Kate, Jane, and Christy, describing the smells and discomfort.  Wendy's screams were highlighted.  Steve from FoundryMusic.com mentioned online video footage.  The hosts discussed the lingering smell, requiring a body deodorizer.
