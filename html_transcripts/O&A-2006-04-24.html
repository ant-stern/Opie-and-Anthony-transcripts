SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast began with a momentous announcement: their return to terrestrial radio on Free FM, simulcast on XM Satellite Radio.  The show, originating from Howard Stern's old studio, featured Opie, Anthony, and Jimmy Norton. They discussed the secrecy surrounding the deal, the premature leak by a website commenter known as "Loudmouth Steve," and their calculated media rollout.  The hosts detailed their new schedule, which included a daily commute between Free FM and XM studios, promising comedic potential from potential mishaps.  The conversation extensively covered their firing from CBS Radio following the infamous "St. Patrick's incident" (with a surprising mention of Bill Donahue's surprisingly supportive statement regarding their return), their subsequent time on XM, and their complex relationship with Howard Stern – acknowledging his influence while playfully teasing him. They listed the cities and stations they'd broadcast on (New York, Philadelphia, Boston, Dallas, Cleveland, Pittsburgh, and West Palm Beach), reminiscing about past incidents in each location, including a prank call about a Boston mayor's death. The broadcast included numerous listener calls, both supportive and critical.  Studio guests included longtime producer Club Soda Kenny, Eric Logan (from XM), and Joel Hollander (from CBS)— a symbolic representation of their dual broadcasting arrangement—along with their agent, Super Agent Bob Eatman.  The segment concluded with further strategizing about their new venture.  The hosts frequently used offensive language and discussed controversial topics throughout.

The show continued, focusing heavily on listener calls celebrating their return after a four-year absence. Many callers hadn't heard the show in years and expressed enthusiastic excitement, some using their online forum nicknames.  Recurring themes included their previous firing and triumphant return; listener reactions (both positive and negative);  recollections of past stunts and contests, notably a disgusting clam chowder-eating contest with a "vomit consumption" component; the dedicated online fanbase, "ONA Pests"; and the transition from their previous satellite radio home to their current terrestrial and satellite arrangement.  The discussion frequently circled back to their relationship with Howard Stern, with callers expressing varying opinions, and mentioned other radio personalities like JV and Elvis. The segment also included plugs for the hosts' personal projects and a restaurant owned by one of the hosts' brothers.  A humorous exchange involving a malfunctioning CD and staff member E-Rock punctuated the segment. The hosts reiterated their future broadcasting schedule encompassing both terrestrial and satellite broadcasts.

Finally, the broadcast included a short segment where the hosts received congratulations, implied to be related to a positive blood test result.  The conversation then quickly shifted to planning a game of "Howie Baseball," dependent on studio preparation by crew members (Danny, Nathaniel, Travis, Derek, Steve, E-Rock, and Ben).  They announced their return to both XM and terrestrial radio, acknowledging the scheduling challenges inherent in this dual arrangement. The segment ended with repeated pronouncements of "The Opie and Anthony Program" followed by Nine Inch Nails' "Head Like a Hole."
