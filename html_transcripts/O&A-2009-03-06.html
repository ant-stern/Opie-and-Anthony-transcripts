SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with a comedic bit imagining a zombie apocalypse, speculating that the homeless would surprisingly fare better than the wealthy.  This segued into a far less hypothetical, and significantly more disastrous, real-life investment story involving the hosts.  Opie and Anthony, joined by Rich Voss (filling in for Jim Norton), recounted their catastrophic investment in the Citadel Broadcasting IPO.  They each invested $100,000, watching their initial investment briefly rise to around $22 per share before collapsing to a single penny.  This meant their combined $200,000 investment was reduced to approximately $55.  The hosts' reactions were laced with profanity and exaggerated expressions of disbelief and anger at their loss, focusing their ire on Citadel's management, Fareed and Judy.  They also revealed that this investment debacle coincided with their subsequent firing from their radio jobs. The segment featured numerous humorous interjections and listener calls, including a particularly cruel prank call where a listener was initially overjoyed at winning $100,000 only to be informed the prize was now worth a measly $55 due to the plummeting Citadel stock. The segment ended with continued comedic reactions and listener interaction.
