SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show opened with their new "F You Friday" segment, a free-for-all of listener-submitted insults and angry rants.  Callers unleashed a torrent of profanity-laced tirades targeting ex-lovers, bosses, coworkers, and even politicians like Boris Yeltsin.  The hosts also read aloud numerous faxes and emails echoing the same sentiments.  One memorable caller, "Eric the elevator man," fumed about his boss denying him a birthday off. Another ripped into Mets fans for booing Mike Piazza.  A female caller’s anger was directed at her ex-boyfriend's insincere apology.  Listeners submitted extremely vulgar messages, one detailing a particularly scathing rant against a thief.  Another caller complained about her husband’s family and their upcoming Russian Orthodox wedding.

The hosts shifted gears to discuss the perceived failings of weather forecasters in their response to Hurricane Bonnie, transitioning into a humorous exchange about Anthony's encounter with a van overloaded with carpet, mimicking a road rage scenario.  A listener called in to criticize the show’s excessive profanity, prompting Opie to coin the nonsensical word "kunk" and engage in a heated argument with another caller defending the initial complaint.  The show included a tribute to the recently deceased composer of the Mighty Mouse theme song.  A segment replayed a previous bit mocking a former boss's reaction to their on-air commentary about a Volkswagen commercial they interpreted as featuring a gay couple.  The broadcast also included a Pearl Jam ticket giveaway promotion, interspersed with the playing of various rock songs throughout the entire segment.
