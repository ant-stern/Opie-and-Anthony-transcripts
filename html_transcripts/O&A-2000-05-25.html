SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show's summer kickoff broadcast from the South Beach Dance Club in Island Park was a raucous affair.  The show, heavily centered around a "Wow Wet T-Shirt Contest," featured a large, enthusiastic crowd displaying numerous offensive signs.  The broadcast was punctuated by constant vulgar language and explicit references to sex and female anatomy, both during the contest commentary and in unrelated segments.  

Musical guests included Fuel (performing "Baby Raper" and “Camp Concentration”), Q (performing "Horny for Little Girls"), Anthony's brother Joe (performing the highly offensive "Just Boned My Daughter" and "Oh My Sewer," the latter plagued by technical difficulties), Stephen Lynch (performing "FBA," an extended collaborative version, and a song about a gerbil), and Billy and Paul from AirSick (performing "Kill a Fetus").  Madison, known for her previous interactions with vegetables, was a prominent guest, teasing the audience throughout the show.  Other guests included Alexis and Tracy (wet t-shirt contestants), Mikey (co-author of "FBA"), and Madison (from the Privileged Gentleman's Club).  Spaz was repeatedly dunked in an ice-filled dunk tank.  

The hosts, Opie and Anthony,  commented extensively on the crowd, the contest participants, the event's technical difficulties (including audio issues affecting Joe's performance), and the pervasive distribution of marijuana among the audience.  Numerous commercial breaks, primarily for Rockaway Betts mattresses, were interspersed with further sexually explicit commentary.  Psycho Mark and Old Dirty Bastard also made appearances (though specifics of their contributions aren't detailed). The show's radio broadcast concluded, but the event continued live at the venue, including the wet t-shirt contest finals and more performances.  The entire event was documented with photos uploaded to the WNEW website in real time.  Numerous sponsors and crew members received thanks at the end of the radio broadcast.
