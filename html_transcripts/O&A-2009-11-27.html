SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast detailed the 2006 (or a later year, as a 4th annual event is referenced) "Homeless Shopping Spree" at the Short Hills Mall in New Jersey.  The broadcast began with E-Rock and Sam Roberts recounting the planning and execution of the event, hampered by a Reverend Al Sharpton-led protest march that caused significant traffic delays in Manhattan.  This resulted in difficulties locating several recurring "homeless friends" of the show, including Papa Rock, Blue (deceased), Half a Hulk Face, Slow Motion, Grey Wolf, and Rich Voss, as well as a new participant, Homeless Fez from the Ron and Fez show, and several other unnamed individuals.  Anthony, in particular, expressed extreme anger and used strong profanity regarding the march and Sharpton.

The show then transitioned to a live broadcast from the studio as a bus carrying the gathered homeless participants journeyed to the Short Hills Mall.  Numerous callers reported massive crowds, estimated to exceed two thousand people, gathered at the mall awaiting the bus.  Listeners provided video and pictures via Paltalk.  The bus journey was extensively documented, with updates from individuals on board, including Rich Voss, who performed stand-up comedy.  Reports detailed heavy traffic, the Lincoln Tunnel crossing (accompanied by humorous commentary about the smell and behavior of the passengers), spotty communication, and anecdotes about various passengers, including Papa Rock and Grey Wolf, whose original song, "Shelter Me," was played.  Grey Wolf's song and a later song, "Homeless for the Holidays," recorded by the participants, were both played on the broadcast.

Upon arrival, the disembarking of the participants was described as chaotic and exciting, met with cheers from the massive crowd.  The show then shifted to relaying events at the mall, focusing on the overwhelming crowds and the homeless individuals' shopping sprees.  Multiple on-site reporters (Fez, Danny, Rich Voss, Than, Kenny, Roland, Steve, Sam, and others) provided updates, detailing purchases ranging from practical items to more extravagant choices.  Several stores temporarily closed due to the overwhelming number of people.  There were numerous humorous anecdotes, including a homeless man repeatedly trying on and returning shoes, and the difficulties navigating the crowds.  A major portion of the broadcast focused on an attempt to get a photo of "Half a Hulk Face" with Santa Claus, which involved negotiations with mall staff and the eventual closure of mall entrances by security and police. A caller expressed significant displeasure with the event, leading to a heated exchange with the hosts.  The strong smell emanating from the participants was repeatedly referenced throughout the broadcast.

The show's live broadcast from the mall involved multiple cameras and phone calls, suffering from numerous technical difficulties and frequent audio problems.  Big Red, a staff member, fell over at some point.  There were reports of confrontations at various stores, including the Gap.  Mall employees and some shoppers expressed discontent.  The broadcast concluded with the homeless individuals boarding the bus back to New York City, along with farewell messages and promises to post photos and videos online.  Throughout the broadcast, numerous callers provided updates and commentary on both the positive and negative reactions of mall patrons.  The entire broadcast featured frequent use of explicit language and profanity by the hosts, callers, and participants.
