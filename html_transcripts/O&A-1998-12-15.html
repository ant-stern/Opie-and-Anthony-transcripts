The Opie and Anthony Show opened with a discussion about the death of Mr. Roper from *Three's Company*, featuring humorous and crude speculation about his sexuality and the show's suggestive undertones. The hosts detailed the club's rules and purpose—reviving intimacy in relationships—and took calls from listeners describing their successes and seeking advice on engaging their partners.  Anthony interspersed the calls with snippets of 70s TV theme songs, particularly various versions of the *Alice* theme song, prompting a discussion about Linda Lavin's singing.  He also lamented his failed investment in Zoom.com stock.

One caller's participation in the Hummer Club led to a lengthy segment where Opie and Anthony called the caller's wife, employing suggestive and explicit language to encourage her participation.  A representative from Vital Transportation then joined the show, advertising their limousine services and offering a giveaway. Opie then played a recording of a previous call where he broke the news to a child that Santa Claus wasn't real.  The show concluded with final remarks about the Hummer Club and the day's events, followed by a Pepsi One advertisement.
