SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with a game centered around audio clips from the HBO documentary "King Gimp." Listeners guessed what the King Gimp was saying, choosing from absurd and often offensive multiple-choice answers.  Contestants easily won prizes including concert tickets to various artists (Red Hot Chili Peppers, Foo Fighters, Adam Ferrara, Stephen Lynch, Green Day, Dr. Dre/Snoop Dogg/Ice Cube/Eminem).  One caller criticized the segment as tasteless and heartless, a criticism briefly acknowledged before the game resumed.  This transitioned into a Mike Tyson impersonation laced with profanity and offensive jokes targeting the British Royal Family and others.  This was followed by playing Orson Welles' profane outtakes from a commercial recording session, with further discussion and a brief *Flintstones* reference.

Andrew Dice Clay then appeared as a guest.  The conversation focused on comedian Jay Moore allegedly performing Dice Clay's material, including his "nicey-nice" bit, on another radio show.  Dice Clay, believing Anthony was involved, called in angrily.  This led to a lengthy, chaotic discussion with accusations, denials, wrestling references, a "meek inheriting the earth" joke, a "boogaloo" running gag, and Jay Moore also joining the phone conversation.  The discussion shifted to Opie's new home gym, with callers mocking Opie and Anthony's reactions.  The segment ended with a failed attempt to get an Elvis impersonator to sing in the studio bathroom, due to the running gag about Elvis' death; the impersonator refused, leading to frustration and the playing of the impersonator's CD.

Next, a phone call with Joe Pesci featured his original rap, "Wiseguy."  This transitioned into a discussion about Louie Anderson's comedic style and his upcoming *Family Feud* hosting gig, referencing Ray Combs' tragic fate and jokingly imagining a *Family Feud* episode based on Anderson's childhood abuse.  A significant portion involved a bizarre extended bit on a catfight promotion, including an interview with a Crystal Films representative detailing the rules (no eye-gouging or face-punching) and the ages of participants, including underage teens.  Opie claimed the ability to guess someone's age from their "taint," attempting (and failing) a demonstration on a female guest.  The segment concluded with Arnold Schwarzenegger prank calls and clips of a live catfight outside the studio.
