SHOW SUMMARY:

The March 10th, 2000, Opie and Anthony Show was a chaotic blend of planned segments and unplanned mayhem.  The broadcast opened with a discussion of the "Most Offensive Song Ever Written" contest, highlighting listener submissions like "Baby Raper" and "Kill a Fetus,"  which sparked significant controversy among listeners and management.  The hosts announced a live roadshow in April where the top entries would be performed live.  A Mountain Dew Final Four promotion was also interwoven throughout.

A major subplot involved the ongoing, disastrous attempts by staff members Spaz and Stinky to fill a kiddie pool with warm water for a planned "Smelly Karen" sponge bath.  This segment, along with the offensive song contest, dominated much of the show, punctuated by numerous listener calls, many containing "F.U." messages and vulgar commentary on both the songs and Spaz and Stinky's comical incompetence.  Stephen Lynch's "Kill a Kitten" was mentioned as another source of listener complaints.  Anthony briefly discussed his previous night's appearance on another show, and a Stacker 2 commercial aired.  The "FU Friday" segment allowed listeners to air grievances, further fueling the already volatile atmosphere.  The "Bit of the Week" contest featured entries including "Kill a Fetus" and a Jamaican diarrhea bit, sparking further discussion about the offensiveness of "Baby Raper."  One caller even recounted a story about infidelity.  The hosts also discussed their personal finances, with one revealing a lucrative past on Wall Street.

The planned "Smelly Karen" segment, involving a listener encouraged not to shower, took a dramatic turn.  Spaz's failure to secure participants for the sponge bath led to his sudden disappearance from the studio, triggering a frantic search involving calls to listeners and a comedic, Tom Brokaw-style parody police bulletin.  Unconfirmed reports of Spaz taking a hostage circulated amidst the search.  Despite Spaz's absence, the "Smelly Karen" segment proceeded, albeit unexpectedly.  Smelly Karen was eventually joined in the kiddie pool by two strippers from Privilege, Stella and Nikita, resulting in a prolonged, sexually explicit scene involving soaping, washing, and mutual touching.  The hosts and callers provided a running, often crude and offensive commentary throughout.  The song "Baby Raper" by Jeff (a.k.a. Fuel) was played, fitting into the ongoing offensive song contest.  The show also featured a mention of Louis C.K.'s previous appearance.

The later part of the show included Dice's return from vacation.  The "Bit of the Week" featured a humorous segment about a lesbian bath, with plans to bottle and distribute the bathwater at the upcoming roadshow.  The "What Did We Learned" segment focused heavily on listener reactions to a webcam feed showing naked women in the pool, using highly explicit language.  Spaz's absence continued to be a running joke, with a fake "Spaz" calling in.  Callers also discussed their reactions to the child rape song, and one caller detailed marital problems.  Listener Billy suggested "Trey Gash" as a name for the bottled bathwater.  Steve from the unofficial O&A website was in the studio documenting the event.  The show concluded with plugs for Anthony's limousine service, an upcoming Louis Black show, and a song summarizing the broadcast.
