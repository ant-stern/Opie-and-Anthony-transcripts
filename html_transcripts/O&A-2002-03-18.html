SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a game where listeners guessed Anthony's desired concert ticket.  Incorrect guesses, like Britney Spears and NSYNC, prompted humorous exchanges, before a caller correctly identified No Doubt, winning WWF tickets. This led to a discussion, fueled by listener calls, about Anthony's surprisingly extensive collection of pop music CDs, contradicting his public persona.

The show then transitioned into "Black Rock Coalition Radio" hosted by Earl Douglas. This segment was characterized by the use of explicit racial slurs, offensive jokes, and racist humor from both the hosts and callers, interspersed with music from rock artists of color.

Following this, the program featured Dr. Allison, an ex-Playboy model and family practice physician, who discussed her experience on *Temptation Island* and the awkward realities of medical examinations.  This segment also included a preview of the "What the Hell Is That?" segment, which immediately followed.

"What the Hell Is That?" involved callers presenting various unusual medical conditions for diagnosis by several doctors in the studio.  Danielle revealed a third nipple (supernumerary nipple), potentially capable of lactation.  Mike displayed a scrotal skin tag, leading to a humorous suggestion for a "Cannibal Day" segment.  Heath described sweating only on one side of his face post-motorcycle accident; John reported chest fluid leakage; and Mel presented a large inner thigh growth.  Each case prompted medical discussion, often graphic and humorous, alongside Jim Norton's reactions to medical textbooks and a running joke about a possibly diseased couch.  Finally, Anthony revealed rotting corneas, leading to discussion of causes and treatments.  The segment concluded with mentions of upcoming show events and the "Sandy Kane" segment.

The Pete segment involved a man with a concerning jaw lump examined by in-studio doctors, including David Wilk.  Concerns about cancer and the need for an ENT specialist were raised, fueled by Pete's smoking history.  Listener Lori and “Mel,” described as looking like "a potato" with a tongue lump, also contributed.

The Sandy Kane segment featured the former stripper and public access host.  The hosts and doctors examined large genital warts on her abdomen, emphasizing the need for medical attention despite Sandy’s skepticism.  She then performed a song, "My Boyfriend's Black," referencing controversial figures. Listener calls punctuated this segment.  The broadcast concluded with an OJ Simpson 911 prank call.
