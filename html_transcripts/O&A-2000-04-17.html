SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with the hosts struggling to edit a station ID recorded by Jimmy "Superfly" Snuka, whose pronunciation of "1027 WNEW" proved exceptionally challenging.  This was followed by the airing of a highly profane audio clip of former Baltimore Orioles manager Earl Weaver responding to fan questions with a barrage of curses and insults.

A caller, Donnie Brosco, then recounted his unlikely discovery of both the Opie and Anthony Show and God while incarcerated.  Another caller provided a description of a billboard in Times Square. The broadcast continued with entries from their "Most Offensive Song Contest," highlighting a particularly graphic and disturbing submission titled "Stuck in an Oven with Juice," submitted anonymously by "The Maestro." This song contained extremely offensive language and imagery related to the Holocaust.  Callers reacted to the song with a mixed response, ranging from indifference to amusement.

The show concluded with the "What Did We Learn Today?" segment, featuring sarcastic and humorous commentary from callers regarding the difficulties with Snuka's station ID, the overall chaotic nature of the broadcast, and the noticeable absence of their usual phone screener, "Spaz."  Outtakes from a drug commercial featuring Psycho Mark were played, showcasing the stark contrast between his sober and intoxicated voiceovers. The broadcast ended with further chaotic and offensive comments from the hosts.
