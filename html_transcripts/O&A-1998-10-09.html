SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show's "FU Friday" broadcast was a raucous affair heavily driven by listener-submitted "FUs," ranging from petty grievances about ex-boyfriends and disliked films like *A Night at the Roxbury* to far more serious complaints, such as a listener recounting a robbery at gunpoint.  The hosts interwove these listener calls with their own brand of commentary.  Their opinions on the new *Columbo* series were mixed, criticizing the actor's age and slower pace, while teasing a future "Dirty Columbo" segment.  They also heavily mocked the New York Yankees' struggles and the team's psychologist's presence at practice.

The ongoing Clinton-Lewinsky scandal provided ample comedic fodder, with jokes and predictions about its popularity as a Halloween costume choice; the hosts strongly discouraged anyone from dressing as either Bill Clinton or Monica Lewinsky.  A significant portion of the show was dedicated to the "Bit of the Week," a prank call to a person named Red involving numerous transfers and increasingly ridiculous excuses.  This proved to be a hit with listeners.

The hosts also revisited a previous bit about a Volkswagen commercial they interpreted as featuring a gay couple, playing a recording of their subsequent conversation with their boss, Dave, who sternly warned them about venturing into "the gay zone."  A recurring listener request for "pork jokes" was eventually fulfilled by playing a recording of a Chinese food delivery person listing various pork dishes while cycling through busy traffic.

The show was punctuated by numerous sexual innuendos and offensive language.  No guests appeared in studio.  Listener participation was a major element, with contests to win Aerosmith tickets based on song identification.  Throughout the broadcast, the hosts maintained a consistent tone of irreverent humor and shock value, fueled by both listener submissions and their own spontaneously generated material.
