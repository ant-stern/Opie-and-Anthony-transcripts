The Opie and Anthony Show's first live broadcast from WYSP in Philadelphia opened with chaotic banter and highly offensive language.  Two WYSP "ambassadors" presented gifts. Immediately, the show was flooded with overwhelmingly negative listener feedback, criticizing the lack of music and the hosts' juvenile humor, often using homophobic and other offensive language themselves.  The hosts, comparing this reaction to their New York debut, predicted a listener "purge" and celebrated their new contract by calling their seemingly intoxicated program director, Tim Sabian, who would repeatedly call throughout the broadcast expressing increasing distress at the negative listener response and sheer volume of angry calls.

A significant portion of the show involved listener calls, ranging from furious rejection to tentative acceptance. A call from a regular New York listener offered encouragement to the Philadelphia audience.  Melissa, a WYSP employee, was jokingly accused of an affair with their boss. The broadcast concluded with more listener reactions, further commentary on "Laverne and Shirley," promotional material for sponsors, and a comedic song from their archives.  Numerous commercials, including one for Bud Light and Diamond Vision, aired throughout the broadcast.
