The Opie and Anthony Show opened with Anthony recounting his lazy Sunday recovery from a Saturday wedding, quickly segueing into a heated debate about a controversial Jets game and the shortcomings of instant replay.  This led to a plug for their website's "instant feedback" feature and a Take Paws pet adoption campaign.  The hosts then critiqued VH1's "Behind the Music," mocking its formulaic nature and repetitive stock footage, before shifting to MTV's "Fanatic," specifically targeting a Hanson-obsessed fan.  Anthony confessed to his late-night Half-Life gaming addiction, prompting Opie's concern about his increasing isolation.

Listener feedback was addressed, followed by Anthony's disgruntled account of poor wedding service. The "Ugly Bride" segment then commenced, a listener contest to guess which bride in the Newsday wedding section Opie and Anthony deemed the ugliest.  Discussion afterward turned to previous guest Stephen Lynch, featuring snippets of his potentially offensive songs, including one about the Special Olympics.

A commercial break promoted "Cigars Around the World," a cigar-of-the-month club.
