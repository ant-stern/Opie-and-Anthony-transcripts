SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show's ninth-anniversary broadcast on SiriusXM was a chaotic and often offensive affair.  The show began with Opie and Anthony, along with Jim Norton, reminiscing about their early days on the network. They recounted their low initial listenership (under 10,000 weekly), their placement on a premium channel, and humorous conflicts, including a feud with a coworker nicknamed "Oompa Loompa" over studio space.  They also detailed awkward technical difficulties during their first broadcast and played audio clips highlighting these issues.  The anniversary segment included numerous anecdotes, such as a clash with building staff and John Valby's inappropriate singing in a piano store.  The conversation frequently digressed into personal details, such as Opie’s daughter's birthday and Norton’s sleep schedule, all while employing copious amounts of vulgar and offensive language. Norton, in a "charming" moment, even referenced "the brutal murder of Russian school children."  The segment ended with a discussion of Miriam Carey ramming her car into the White House security barrier.

The discussion then shifted to Carey’s actions, with the hosts engaging in highly offensive speculation about her mental state and motives.  They moved on to the government shutdown, focusing on unpaid "essential" employees.  An Alex Jones segment was played, where he labeled the White House incident a "false flag" operation, sparking further conspiracy theories from the hosts about mind control and Manchurian candidates. This transitioned into a discussion about the U.S. debt ceiling, laced with racist and xenophobic comments about Chinese people.  A humorous anecdote about the Bugs Bunny character Finster and edited Looney Tunes cartoons followed, before the conversation returned to politics with a focus on hot mic recordings of Rand Paul and Mitch McConnell, leading to more offensive and bigoted remarks.  A major turning point occurred when Opie accidentally, or possibly intentionally, revealed co-worker Eric ("E-Rock")'s phone number, resulting in a deluge of calls and abusive messages. The hosts' attempts to use the dump button failed, adding to the chaos.  A former acquaintance of a female caller, "Irving," then called in, recounting a past sexual encounter with her when she was a minor.  The segment concluded with frantic attempts to contact E-Rock and discussions about Miley Cyrus and Opie's commitment to compensating E-Rock for the phone number incident.

The fallout from E-Rock's exposed number dominated the next segment. The hosts discussed the flood of offensive messages, many targeting E-Rock's weight, and speculated on his reaction.  Listeners' tweets and screenshots of messages were discussed, showing images depicting E-Rock as a chubby baby and fake contest entries.  Opie offered to pay for E-Rock’s Disney World vacation (excluding meals) as an apology.  The hosts debated whether E-Rock was genuinely upset or seeking attention. The segment concluded with the transition to "Chucktober," a planned segment about radio failures, and a call from comedian Dan Soder.

A "Jocktober" segment featured Scott and Todd, whose antagonistic dynamic was a recurring theme.  It started with a nonsensical parody song about taco meat, followed by a discussion of Carrie Underwood's Beatles cover and the resulting negative tweets.  Brian from "Impractical Jokers" called in, mistaking the taco meat song for a new bit and expressed his fandom, receiving an invitation to appear on the show.  The segment included more Scott and Todd banter, Scott's rambling statements about movies (especially "The Boat Movie"), and a heavily edited phone scam segment featuring Sam, an employee at a clothing store, who was revealed to be part of the prank.  The "How Long Before You Tap Out" game was also mentioned, along with further comments on the taco meat song and phone scam segments.  The segment continued to utilize racist, homophobic and other offensive language.

A final segment saw Opie and Anthony mocking a poorly executed phone scam.  Chef Sue Torres from Suenos was then brought in to promote her restaurant.  The hosts, along with Dan Soder, engaged in a lengthy discussion about tacos, including a humorous tangent about fake tuna in restaurants.  Further discussion of the E-Rock phone number incident and speculation about his reaction and potential revenge followed.  The show included numerous plugs for Sue Torres' restaurant, Dan Soder's shows, a cancer benefit, a YouTube video, and a MyPillow commercial.  Dan Soder performed a stand-up bit about Russians and his mugging avoidance techniques.  The show ended with more speculation about E-Rock's response, comments on SiriusXM's Diversity Day (with emphasis on the refreshments), and Opie recounting a negative experience working at a Mexican restaurant. Throughout the entire broadcast, the hosts and callers frequently used highly offensive and inappropriate language.
