SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a raucous discussion of the Bill Clinton-Monica Lewinsky scandal. Opie and Anthony employed crude and sexually explicit humor, detailing their impressions of the affair, the infamous dress, and even engaging in humorous role-playing of conversations between Clinton, Lewinsky, and related individuals.  New photos and videos surfacing of the pair were also a topic of conversation, fueling further sexually explicit jokes. Listeners called in to dedicate songs to Clinton and Lewinsky, creating a comedic musical montage.

The tone shifted somewhat with a discussion of the recent deaths of several children's television personalities, focusing on Shari Lewis, the creator of Lamb Chop.  Speculation arose regarding a supposed "curse" affecting ventriloquists.  This led to a comedic, albeit vulgar, faux-interview with Lamb Chop, where the puppet character unleashed a tirade of bitter and profane comments directed at Shari Lewis.

Opie then shared details of his personal life, revealing his chaotic living situation: paying rent on two apartments while residing on his brother's couch.  He recounted a humorous anecdote about receiving a telemarketing call shortly after setting up a new phone line.  Announcements followed, promoting upcoming musical guests for the show's "Imaginary Ballroom" segment: Third Eye Blind, Semisonic, and Fastball.  The broadcast concluded with a listener prank call segment, featuring a daughter calling her mother in Montana, pretending to be working in the pornography industry.
