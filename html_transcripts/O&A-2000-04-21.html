SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast was a wild ride, opening with a "F.U. Friday" focused on Opie's alleged Valium use.  Sandy, his girlfriend, suspects he's becoming addicted, a fear fueled by his recent back injury and subsequent consumption of leftover prescription Valium, supplemented by a generous supply from a "faithful listener."  This sparked a discussion about Opie's paranoia and Sandy's concerns, interspersed with a running gag about a studio fart, with Ben, a studio employee, bearing the brunt of the suspicion.  Listener calls offered various opinions on Opie's drug use, alongside promotional spots for Stacker 2.  The segment also featured calls from Mike (discussing the O&A/Don and Mike show relationship) and Fudd (from Anthony's band).  Bridget, a listener, repeatedly called to claim there was hidden camera footage of her getting waxed at the station – a claim vehemently denied by the hosts.  Psycho Mark was also mentioned as a studio employee; the Filter band was discussed by callers, with differing opinions on their previous appearance on the show.

The show then shifted to a discussion about the futility of band promotion (Opie recounted anecdotes from his band Rotgut and his brother Joe’s approach), followed by listener calls regarding the band's upcoming gig at Sergeant Peppers.  Humorous discussions about planned song covers, including Christina Aguilera and boy bands, and, controversially, a Columbine High School shooting parody featuring Anthony's brother and sister, took center stage. The song's inclusion prompted a brief debate on its offensiveness, including a call from CNN's Bill, expressing interest in the parody.  This transitioned into a rant about the corporatization of radio, including mandatory sexual harassment training videos, prompting humorous discussions involving on-air and off-air staff, including Gabriel from HR.  A caller, Mark, expressed envy of their jobs, furthering the contrast between their work and "real jobs."  The sexual harassment video became a recurring source of on-air jokes.  The "Bit of the Week" featured Spaz (Kevin), a coworker who called Opie at home, wanting to connect more personally. This led to humorous speculation and a dinner date agreement after the show.  Musical interludes, including jingles and song parodies, punctuated these segments.

Further discussion of the hosts' relationships with coworkers, particularly Spaz, continued, with playful banter about a potential future show just the two of them. This was followed by a segment with Jill, a woman previously met at a bar appearance, who had hot wax dripped onto her body in the studio, generating laughter, pain, and suggestive comments, alongside audience participation via phone calls and website photo discussions. This part also involved handling issues with no-shows from previously scheduled guests.

Numerous commercial breaks significantly impacted the scheduled programming.  The "Bit of the Week" then focused on Spaz's extravagant birthday spending spree, detailing his $625-a-night St. Regis stay, $700 mini-bar bill, and overall $2000 spending in five days on his $5500 credit limit.  The hosts and callers mocked his irresponsible spending, with Anthony sharing his own past credit card debt struggles. The segment also included giving away boxing match tickets, listener feedback on the "hot wax" segment, and commercials for Car Cash and Fidelity Diamonds. Foot, a musician/comedian, was a guest, promoting his performances.  The show ended noting their high ratings, particularly those contributed by their Detroit affiliates.
