SHOW SUMMARY:

The April 25th, 2000, Opie and Anthony Show began with a phone call from their "Southern boss" announcing record-high ratings, making them number one across key demographics.  Despite the celebratory news, the boss sternly warned against future stunts, threatening job termination.  Opie and Anthony, elated by their success, discussed plans to thank listeners with a beer buy-in, encountering logistical hurdles in finding a suitable venue due to pre-existing commitments and closures. They sought listener suggestions.

A substantial portion of the show detailed the previous night's escapades involving Psycho Mark and Stinky.  Stinky's attempt to display a "WOW" sign upside down during live television broadcasts on Channels 7 and 11 resulted in a chase by a cameraman and subsequent apprehension by several police officers near a fundraiser attended by President Clinton, Vice President Gore, and Hillary Clinton. Psycho Mark was also involved and briefly detained. Both Stinky and Psycho Mark were interviewed in-studio, recounting the incident and their injuries, which were the subject of much mockery from the hosts.  The hosts promoted new "WOW" stickers to be distributed by Psycho Mark outside the Brooklyn Diner and teased an upcoming listener appreciation event, hinting at a possible strip club location.

The show continued with further discussion of the ratings success and the ensuing celebration.  The hosts recounted the Psycho Mark and Stinky incident, comparing the adrenaline rush to a drug high.  They mentioned needing to show ID and expressed relief that Black Earl wasn’t involved. The planned listener appreciation party at a strip club was heavily discussed, acknowledging their primarily male audience and attempts to attract more female listeners.  Female callers expressed mixed feelings about the location, with some expressing concerns and others viewing it as potentially fun.  The hosts repeatedly stressed there would be no harassment.  Negotiations with club representatives, relayed through phone calls, determined the cover charge would be waived, a free buffet provided, and the first round of drinks on the show.  Listener calls, discussion about photoshopped images of the Elian Gonzalez situation, and promotion of the “WOW” stickers continued. The event was ultimately confirmed to be at Privilege, a strip club at 23rd and 11th Avenue.

Numerous congratulatory calls followed, featuring rambling, often vulgar, thank-yous from listeners, friends, and associates, including Mike Tyson (who threatened violence if not properly thanked) and Weird Al Yankovic (who offered a joking congratulation).  Other callers used pseudonyms like "The Fart Guy," "Old Dirty," and "Spaz."  The hosts thanked numerous people on and off-air, including past antagonists. The upcoming strip club celebration at Privilege, with a free buffet and drinks, was repeatedly mentioned.  A humorous, yet offensive, Lamb Chop puppet interview was included, along with commercials for Car Cash and Ranch One.  A wrestling pay-per-view and a listener-submitted photo caption contest on foundrymusic.com were also promoted.  The segment concluded with a bizarre phone call involving a listener attempting to get his sister to engage in sexually suggestive acts on air.

The final segment opened with a humorous call about a listener's unsuccessful attempts to find "naked chicks." The hosts reiterated plans for the Privilege event, emphasizing the 21-and-over age restriction.  A major focus was a confrontation with Spaz, a show employee, who refused to attend Privilege due to moving plans. This led to a heated exchange, accusations of Spaz being "pussy whipped," and attempts to get Spaz and his girlfriend, Erica, on the air.  Erica eventually agreed to attend. The broadcast culminated in an in-studio brawl between Spaz and Old Dirty, escalating to include Rick, resulting in Spaz punching Rick and knocking a clock off the wall, which then hit Rick. The fight was detailed with play-by-play commentary from the hosts and callers.  The show concluded with the hosts reiterating their Privilege plans and promising further mayhem on the next show.
