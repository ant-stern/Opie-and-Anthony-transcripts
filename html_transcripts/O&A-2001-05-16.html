SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with a protracted segment focusing on intern Brian, who arrived at work with a dramatic bandage and eye patch, claiming a motorcycle accident.  His inconsistent story, lack of specifics, and past absences fueled suspicion among the hosts and staff.  Despite initially refusing to remove the bandage, citing glass in his eye and doctor's orders, Brian eventually confessed to fabricating the injury.  The bandage and patch were removed, revealing no injuries.  The segment involved numerous listener calls, including one claiming to know Brian's doctor. A listener vote determined Brian would remain, but under continued scrutiny, notably forced to keep wearing the eye patch.  The hosts expressed amusement and frustration.


The fallout from Brian’s deception dominated further discussion, provoking heated debates about his potential dismissal and the breach of trust.  Opie and Anthony utilized the situation for comedic effect, employing mocking and insults.  Multiple calls advocated for Brian’s immediate dismissal.


The show then transitioned to a game where guest Brittany attempted to guess the profession of guest Jay Moore.  Brittany initially believed him to be an intern, leading to humorous misunderstandings.  The reveal of Jay as an actor (with credits including *Jerry Maguire*) provided a comedic conclusion to this segment.


Following this,  the show returned to the Brian saga with additional listener calls, followed by listener calls sharing military anecdotes filled with offensive language targeting various nationalities.  The hosts then mentioned their upcoming Hard Rock Cafe appearance.


A subsequent segment featured a discussion between Opie, Anthony, and producer Spaz (Kevin), who expressed a desire for a closer personal relationship outside of work.  The hosts playfully teased this request. The conversation explored differences in male and female friendships and the awkwardness of forcing a friendship. Reggie, another show employee,  phoned in to speculate on Spaz's motives.  This led to discussion of Spaz's book, his recent birthday (and a listener’s positive review of a birthday card Spaz sent), a past charity event where they were hit with golf balls in hockey gear, difficulties donating to charities (including a story about their general manager, Bruce Mittman, using a derogatory term for the recipients of their donation to the Minority Homeless Veterans Association of Boston), and the fallout from a past on-air segment, "Hi, Mom, I'm Gay," including a playback of the segment and discussion of a subsequent meeting with a gay rights organization and Bruce Mittman's reactions. Chara briefly commented on receiving a birthday card from Spaz.


The broadcast concluded with a recounting of an April Fool's prank gone wrong involving the fabrication of Boston Mayor Menino's death.  The prank resulted in significant backlash and threatened their jobs.  Bruce Mittman ("the midget") attempted damage control, secretly arranging a photo opportunity with the mayor.  Dada Dave devised a further appeasement plan:  Opie and Anthony would be placed in stocks and have pies thrown at them.  Dave ordered 500 pies without consulting them.  Opie and Anthony refused, leading to Dave's anger and a PR strategy backfire.  A revised plan involved taking the pies to a children's cancer hospital.  Upon realizing the children were terminally ill, including a child on life support, Opie and Anthony felt the situation was exploitative, left the pies, and ultimately viewed their subsequent firing as a positive outcome.  The segment contained frequent homophobic slurs and other offensive language.
