SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast on November 14th, 2001, was a whirlwind of vulgarity, outrageousness, and bizarre tangents.  The show began with Opie detailing his explosive anger, culminating in him punching a bulletproof subway token booth window after a frustrating commute, injuring his hand and toe. This sparked a lengthy rant against the perceived incompetence of NYC subway employees, fueled by numerous caller anecdotes about their own negative experiences with the city's transportation system and drivers.  Opie also recounted a previous incident involving a car accident and subsequent damage to his vehicle by the pedestrian he hit.  The discussion was liberally sprinkled with offensive language and descriptions of violent acts. The hosts briefly touched upon a Sam Adams beer commercial contest and the television show *24*.

The conversation then shifted to a scathing, sexually explicit and offensive critique of a Michael Jackson television special. Opie and Anthony, along with caller Marianne, relentlessly mocked Jackson’s appearance, making crude jokes about his physical features and alleged child abuse.  Macaulay Culkin and Corey Feldman were repeatedly mentioned in connection with Jackson.  Liza Minnelli and Elizabeth Taylor also received similarly harsh and offensive criticism.  A clip of Minnelli performing was played, intensifying their mocking commentary. The hosts also displayed and discussed a picture showing Jackson's changing appearance over time.


Following the Jackson segment, the show focused on a planned "Cherry Darts" Xbox contest,  involving throwing darts at a ambiguously located area of a woman's body. Numerous callers, sharing often pathetic and rambling life stories, vied for a chance to participate. One caller's attempt to donate the prize to charity backfired spectacularly. A prank call concluding this segment involved informing a supposed European vacation winner that they'd actually won a set of steak knives.

A subsequent segment showcased callers competing for an Xbox by recounting their "pathetic" life stories.  These stories included relationship failures, job losses, and other misfortunes.  One caller described his girlfriend's infidelity with a firefighter shortly after his job loss, while another recounted a car door injury. This segment also heavily featured sound clips and discussion of a "Weakest Link" episode with WWE wrestlers, particularly Booker T's repeated incorrect answer, "Captain Kangaroo,"  with Big Show and Lita also mentioned.  A 19-year-old caller, Jen, a pot smoker, shared her story, engaging in playful banter with the hosts.

Comedian Christopher Titus then called in to promote his show. Opie and Anthony played a profanity-laden promotional spot they’d created, highlighting the show's dysfunctional family themes and dark humor. Titus discussed his show's premise, upcoming episodes, his experiences with Fox, and commented on "Malcolm in the Middle," specifically referencing a brief breast exposure scene.  A news report about a topless woman outside Madison Square Garden offering oral sex to Rangers game attendees was followed by a brief, obscenity-filled phone call with the woman herself, concluding with a Stacker 2 commercial.

A "Mr. DVD" segment, featuring a Netflix representative, reviewed upcoming DVD releases, including *Shrek*, *Planet of the Apes*, *The Grinch*, *Apocalypse Now*, *The Godfather*, *Almost Famous*, and *Go*.  Mr. DVD detailed special features and the discussion included critiques of Mark Wahlberg's acting and a humorous exchange about a Shrek doll. Callers' questions and off-topic comments led to further comedic exchanges. The segment also included commercials for Neil Toyota and the Vermont Teddy Bear Company's anniversary sale, as well as mentions of the upcoming "Cherry Darts" game. The hosts also promoted a heavy metal comedy tour.
