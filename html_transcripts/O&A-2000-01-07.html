SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opens with a caller launching a furious, obscenity-laced tirade against the hosts and their content, culminating in a threat to visit the radio station.  This is followed by a comedic bit: a parody of O.J. Simpson making absurd and inappropriate 911 calls.

The majority of the show centers on the aftermath of a contentious interview with The Rock.  The hosts, visibly angry, dissect The Rock's abrupt departure from the interview, triggered by offensive material on a website associated with a guest, Sick Boy.  They vehemently deny knowledge of the offensive content, arguing that The Rock overreacted and should have addressed his concerns directly.  Studio guest Steve, from the show's unofficial website, reinforces their perspective, providing further details about Sick Boy’s reaction.  Numerous callers join the discussion, expressing divided opinions on the hosts’ handling of the situation and the validity of The Rock’s accusations. The hosts also engage in a debate about freedom of speech online and accuse The Rock of hypocrisy regarding racial stereotypes in light of his own wrestling career in the WWF. A Rouse.com representative calls, promising to send a nude model to the studio for the scheduled guest, Lewis Black (who, however, does not appear during this portion of the show).  The arrest of Rosie Perez for protesting nuclear testing in Puerto Rico is briefly mentioned.

The program continues with a discussion of a previous rock show segment, transitioning into a detailed and profane "F-word lesson," exploring the etymology and grammatical usage of the word "fuck." An email from AJ from Brooklyn is read, highlighting perceived double standards in racial humor.

Comedian Lewis Black then joins the show.  Their conversation covers Black's recent activities (including skiing to avoid family time), his experiences performing around the holidays, and his perspective on the Y2K scare and the excessive security measures surrounding the millennium. They discuss the lack of actual Y2K problems and the widespread societal paranoia.  A call from Jay Moore in Hollywood follows, discussing the show's increasing popularity, referencing a previous, successful segment involving gay phone sex.  Moore recounts awkward encounters with Hollywood directors, highlighting the industry's absurdity and his own frustrations.  The conversation, peppered with offensive language, circles back to The Rock.  The discussion returns to Lewis Black, focusing on the overreaction to Y2K, disappointing show attendance, increased surveillance, and the absurdity of certain news coverage of the Y2K event.  The segment ends with mention of a promised appearance by a naked woman from a website and plugs for various sponsors, including Paisano's restaurant. Profanity, crude humor, and offensive language are prevalent throughout.

A later segment features a discussion about Rouse.com, with Christy, a Miami Dolphins cheerleader and editor for the site, as a studio guest. Crude and offensive language and sexually suggestive humor persist.

A humorous bulk email detailing "17 ways women fail in bed" is discussed, followed by a brief, crude description of its contents. The show concludes abruptly during the introduction of the "Bit of the Week," focusing on a newspaper photo of Ricky Martin with a male companion on a beach, before the bit is fully revealed.  Throughout the entire broadcast, profanity, crude humor, and offensive language are frequent.
