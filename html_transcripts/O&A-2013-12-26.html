The Opie and Anthony Show’s “Worst of 2013” broadcast, as pieced together from various excerpts, was a chaotic and heavily intoxicated affair.  The show opened with E-Rock introducing a particularly wild episode featuring several prominent guests.  Jeffrey Tambor was interviewed, discussing his acting career, including his roles in *Arrested Development* and *The Ropers*, sharing anecdotes about Al Pacino and his experiences on set.  Mick Foley joined the conversation, a fellow *Arrested Development* fan, gifting Tambor a Santa Claus t-shirt.  This segued into a segment imagining a perpetually drunk Jim Norton, followed by a detailed description of Norton's superhero creation, "Grocery Man," with his unique grocery-related powers.

Courtney Love was interviewed next, joined by Jim Florentine in studio.  She discussed Amanda Bynes, her music industry experiences (including refusing to lip-sync at the Billboard Awards), her relationship with Eric Erlandson (who possessed many of Kurt Cobain's guitars), and Anthony Kiedis.  The conversation also touched on auto-tune and the ongoing discussion about "Grocery Man."  Love detailed a dispute over Kurt Cobain's belongings, accusing the EMP Museum of mishandling and auctioning them off improperly, comparing it to the theft of artwork during the Holocaust. She recounted a bizarre encounter with an EMP curator, discussed the location of Cobain’s ashes, and her strained relationship with Dave Grohl, focusing on financial issues related to Nirvana Inc.  She cryptically hinted at a potential role as a judge on a major television show.

Later in the show, Louis C.K. appeared. He discussed his upcoming season of "Louie," his year-long break, his creative process (comparing it to farming), and a humiliating encounter with Nicolas Cage on a plane.  He detailed the production of his stand-up special, including filming in the round with Alexa cameras and the editing challenges. The conversation included his family life, interactions with fans, and his recent purchase of a 42-foot boat.

The show then shifted to a rant about SiriusXM’s confusing premium channel charges and poor customer service, with the hosts vowing to cancel their subscriptions. This transitioned into a broader discussion of media companies’ frustrating content restrictions and international availability issues, particularly referencing piracy in Australia.

An unexpected in-studio guest, Roger Daltrey, discussed his Teen Cancer America charity, focusing on specialized care for teenagers with cancer and providing the website for donations.
