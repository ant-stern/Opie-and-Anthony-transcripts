The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast detailed their chaotic experience at the MTV Video Music Awards (VMAs).  The centerpiece of the show revolved around their stunt of sending two women, referred to repeatedly and offensively as "retarded Laverne and Shirley" or "Lady Di and Marion," to conduct interviews on the red carpet.  This resulted in a series of comically inept and often offensive interactions with numerous celebrities, including Janet Jackson, Mick Jagger, Will Ferrell, Alicia Keys, Little Moe, Nate Dogg (mistakenly identified as Kato Kaelin), the Backstreet Boys, Ja Rule, Fatboy Slim, Dale Earnhardt Jr., and  John Bon Jovi and Richie Sambora (whose strong reactions were a recurring gag).  Audio clips of these interviews were played throughout the broadcast.  The hosts discussed the varying levels of celebrity engagement with the press, ranging from enthusiastic to outright avoidance.  They recounted their own past experiences at award shows, including being ejected from a Grammys after-party.

The show also featured a mix of in-studio guests and phone calls.  Guests included Kimmy from *Survivor* (Australia), described as a "nympho freak," who engaged in sexually suggestive banter;  Mike from *Real World: New York*, who appeared in multiple segments sharing anecdotes from the VMAs after-party, including one involving Christina Aguilera; Mario Cantone; and musician John Valby, who performed several songs with explicit lyrics.
