SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with Sam Roberts hosting the "Worst of the Week Countdown," showcasing highlights from the week's interviews.  This included in-studio appearances by Adewale Agbaje (Adebisi from *Oz*, promoting *Thor*), who recounted humorous anecdotes about filming *Thor* in Iceland, particularly the fight choreography; Jason Statham (promoting *Homefront*), who discussed his career, a reported feud between Bruce Willis and Sylvester Stallone, and his past reckless driving; and Julius "Dr. J" Erving, who promoted his autobiography, discussed his career highlights, including a recent dunk at 63, and his surprisingly modest NBA salary despite giving away millions.  Other guests mentioned in this segment included Bill Burr, Colin Quinn, and interviews with Oliver Stone and Guillermo del Toro.  The segment featured frequent coarse language and humor, notably including an on-air incident where a caller, “Lady Di,” inadvertently revealed her phone number on Twitter. The hosts attempted to help her rectify this, leading to a chaotic and humorous sequence resulting in her number being widely disseminated.

Dr. J's extended interview delved into his early career struggles despite impressive college stats, his time playing overseas, the origin of his nickname "Dr. J," his financial decisions including significant charitable giving and some bad investments, his relationship with a woman named Samantha and the resulting child support arrangements, and a comparison of his upbringing to that of his children.

Guillermo del Toro's in-studio appearance covered his childhood love of monster movies, a memorable incident involving urine, his “monster house” with secret passages and a “Rain Room,” and his father's 72-day kidnapping in Mexico. Del Toro also humorously discussed his strategic purchase of the house next door to avoid neighborhood suspicions about alleged porn production.

Oliver Stone's scheduled appearance was significantly delayed, leading to humorous debate among the hosts and Sam Roberts about whether to wait.  Ultimately, they left the studio early due to scheduling conflicts, recalling previous instances where they left before guests like Ricky Gervais and James Cromwell arrived.  However, Stone eventually arrived, and a lengthy post-show interview with Sam Roberts ensued, covering Stone’s documentary "Untold History of the United States," his views on historical figures (particularly Truman and Eisenhower), the JFK assassination, and his filmmaking career.  A final segment included Bill Burr, who joined Sam Roberts for a discussion that included an anecdote about a less-than-enthusiastic encounter with Don Cheadle and a digression about Walt Frazier and Earl Monroe.

The show included several commercial breaks featuring advertisements for Sirius XM, Retro Fitness, credit repair services, Dish Network, Anchor Funding Services, Cheapoware.com, cigars, a matchmaking service, Viagra, tax relief, audiobooks, Dollar Shave Club, business phone systems, weight-loss products, and life insurance.

A "Friends of the Show" segment featured Tom Papa's Sirius XM show, "Come to Papa," which included a racially charged comedic bit with Jim Norton, Kurt Metzger, Keith Robinson, and Godfrey portraying a fictional first jazz band, a comedic performance by Lou Soloff, a tribute to Louis Armstrong with racially charged humor featuring a celebrity impersonator, and a skit involving a jazz club bouncer. Andy Rooney's impersonation concluded the segment.

The broadcast also included a segment with comedian Rich Vos, whose highly profane stand-up set covered various topics, including offensive jokes about midgets, pedophiles, and priests, criticisms of Keith Robinson, jokes about toll prices, Occupy Wall Street, a large bank deposit and resulting "platinum" membership, a bizarre eel story, and male insecurities.  The hosts engaged in banter with Vos throughout his set.  The show ended with Tom Papa’s monologue.  Throughout the entire broadcast, the hosts frequently used coarse language and humor.
