SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show broadcast covered a wide range of topics, often transitioning abruptly between segments.  It began with a discussion of offensive World War II-era anti-Japanese propaganda songs, such as "Remember Pearl Harbor" and "Gonna Have to Slap the Dirty Little Jap," prompting commentary on the contrast between then and now regarding acceptable levels of racism and jingoism in media.  This led into a broader discussion of political correctness in media coverage of war and terrorism, specifically mentioning Fox News's use of "homicide bomber," and airport security.  Listener-submitted audio, including old, racially charged cigarette commercials, further fueled this discussion.  Two callers, John and Al,  phoned in to discuss their humorous musical parody of *Silence of the Lambs*. A public service announcement about the dangers of acid was mentioned but not played in full.

Anthony then launched into a rambling story about a bad acid trip in San Francisco, vividly recounting a hallucination where he mistook a hot dog for his friend Terry's penis, which then appeared to have a face and screamed when Anthony attempted to eat it. This bizarre anecdote segued into a discussion of other drug experiences, including Anthony's mescaline trip while seeing Van Halen.  This was followed by a pre-recorded phone call with William Shatner.  The hosts initially pretended to be unfamiliar with Shatner before playing clips of his spoken-word recordings of songs like "Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds" and "Mr. Tambourine Man." The conversation covered Shatner's musical career, his album "Has Been," and his role in *Boston Legal*, with much playful antagonism between the hosts and Shatner.  "Sex Bagel" Eric conducted a street interview with a Tony Danza fan, who agreed to perform a favor for Danza in exchange for a backstage pass. Jim Norton was present throughout this portion and participated in the discussions.

A significant portion of the show revolved around a prank involving a fake interview with a "Tony Danza show" audience member. Anthony, impersonating Danza, conducted a phone interview with a woman, while Eric was sent to conduct an in-person interview with the same woman. The prank was discovered by the Tony Danza show's head of security, who physically prevented Eric from making further calls, confiscating his phone. This resulted in a chaotic sequence of events, with Eric attempting to flee, the hosts providing commentary and instructions, and much speculation about the security guard's actions and possible legal ramifications.  Crude jokes and comments were made throughout this segment. Eric eventually returned to the studio, recounting his altercation. A caller, "Corinne" or "Donkey Sausage," called in to discuss past interactions with Opie and Anthony. Jim Norton also read excerpts from a book titled "Rent Boys" as punishment for an intern who had brought the wrong brand of coffee.

Further segments included Jim Norton recounting embarrassing childhood bowel control issues, potentially linking them to possible, though un-remembered, childhood sexual abuse. This transitioned into a segment about Frankie Blue, their former station's program director at WNEW.  Audio clips were played of an intoxicated Frankie Blue broadcasting a radio show, slurring his words, mispronouncing the station's call letters, and making inappropriate comments, followed by a discussion of his subsequent firing.  The show concluded with a discussion of a successful publicity stunt involving large signs shown during a Good Morning America broadcast.  The hosts were in different locations (Jim Norton in Los Angeles, Anthony on a cruise, and Opie's location unspecified) but connected via MiFi.  The audio of Frankie Blue's on-air meltdown was a substantial part of the broadcast, interspersed with song snippets, including Donna Summer's "Last Dance."  The show also included a phone call from a concerned mother about her son quitting prostitution and suggesting he take up exercise instead, followed by a comedic bit featuring a caller impersonating Ronald Reagan making sexually suggestive comments while calling a gift shop to inquire about Nancy Reagan.
