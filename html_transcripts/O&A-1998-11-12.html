SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show opened with a giveaway contest for a Bruce Springsteen box set, followed by the playing of tracks from the unreleased *Chef and Friends: The Songs of South Park* CD.  This included memorable performances of  Eric Cartman's rendition of "Come Sail Away" and Chef's "Chocolate Salty Balls."  A call from Aerosmith's Steven Tyler ensued, prompting the reading of a fawning fax from a listener named Phyllis.  The hosts engaged in a discussion about the difficulties of marital fidelity for rock stars.  This transitioned into a lengthy conversation about quitting smoking, with Anthony detailing his personal struggles.

Jim Cook, the personal brewer for Opie and Anthony and founder of Sam Adams, then appeared in studio with a selection of beers, including a Winter Lager and Triple Bock.  Cook shared an anecdote about giving his newborn daughter a taste of Sam Adams beer. Olympic gold medalist Tommy Moe called in, leading to an announcement of a sponsored ski trip to Jackson Hole with Moe, backed by Sam Adams and Dynastar.  The show's giveaway plan for the Bruce Springsteen box set was further elaborated, involving a woman flashing her breasts on the Today Show, with additional stipulations attached.  A listener, Joe, called to declare his participation in the show's "Tuesday Night Hummer Club." The broadcast concluded with further commentary on the *South Park* CD and a visual display of the numerous empty beer bottles accumulated throughout the show.
