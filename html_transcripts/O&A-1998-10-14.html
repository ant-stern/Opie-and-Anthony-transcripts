SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show began by announcing their disastrous summer ratings, dropping from 21st to 24th place.  This news was met with a blend of mock outrage and celebratory absurdity.  Opie and Anthony reacted with disbelief and joked about the insignificance of some of the stations that outranked them.  Gary Blumman, a sales representative, made a brief appearance.  The hosts then hilariously declared a "last place party," demanding a cake, strippers, and party favors.

The broadcast was flooded with listener calls, ranging from congratulatory messages celebrating their low ranking to angry and disappointed reactions.  Several prominent radio personalities, including Bob Grant and Don Imus, chimed in with sarcastic congratulations or outright insults.  A prank caller, impersonating Tom Brokaw of NBC Nightly News, delivered a comically inaccurate and fabricated news report.  The Diceman also joined the show, adding his own brand of mockery to Opie and Anthony's ratings predicament.

Amidst the listener calls and humorous banter, interspersed with music, the hosts continued their comedic responses to the ratings debacle.  Instead of the requested cake, donuts arrived, further fueling the comedic narrative.  They even briefly discussed potential strategies to improve their ratings, such as learning Spanish and increasing listener interaction.  The show concluded with more jokes about their last-place finish and a teaser for the next day's guest, Willie Nelson.
