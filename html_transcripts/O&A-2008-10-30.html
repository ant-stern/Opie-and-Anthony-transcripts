SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast heavily featured the Philadelphia Phillies' World Series victory.  Opie and Anthony, visibly intoxicated, celebrated the win with boisterous, albeit arguably disingenuous, proclamations of their Philly fandom, despite Anthony's admitted Long Island roots.  They reacted to the game's surprisingly short duration, contrasting it with the perceived slow pace of modern baseball, and mocked various commentators, particularly targeting Joe Buck for his perceived lack of excitement.  The hosts played multiple replays of the winning moment, including a humorous clip from a Japanese broadcast.

A significant portion of the show revolved around a rambling, profane, and comedic bit where Opie invented a tough childhood in West Philadelphia. This led to a caller challenging Opie's story due to the area's predominantly non-white demographics.  Opie's response was a series of escalating, absurd, and exaggerated tales, culminating in a  "Fresh Prince of Bel-Air"-style rap about his fictional, hard-knock life in the city.

A repo man called in, providing a firsthand account of the chaotic and celebratory atmosphere in Philadelphia following the Phillies' win.  Later, Jay Moore joined the show, participating in a series of disconnected, often nonsensical and profane, jokes and comedic exchanges with the hosts, including impressions and a movie quote game. The broadcast concluded with a promotional announcement for a future segment showcasing strippers celebrating the release of Kevin Smith's "Zack and Miri Make a Porno."
