SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast opened with the hosts celebrating their continued employment despite listener bets on their imminent firing. Opie recounted a horrific flight from Florida, marked by severe turbulence and widespread vomiting, followed by a chaotic journey home involving a bus and train after a family transportation snafu.  Anthony detailed his holiday experiences, including a lengthy, contentious blackjack session in Atlantic City where he clashed with other players and met a listener, Cory from Staten Island. Both hosts shared anecdotes about the awkwardness of obligatory family holiday kissing.

The show then delved into their "Top 99 Albums of 1999" list, engaging in playful debate and mockery of their own subjective rankings. They briefly, and critically, reviewed New Year's Eve television specials, lambasting the performances and commenting on Dick Clark's aging appearance, particularly criticizing Jesse Ventura's incoherent appearance on MTV's New Year's Eve broadcast, along with Jennifer Love Hewitt's participation.  A segment followed detailing bizarre and tragic 1998 deaths, including a man killed by a pipe on the Long Island Expressway, a Disney boat cleat fatality, a paraplegic who froze to death, and a man who fell down an elevator shaft. A listener called in to contribute another unusual death story.

After advertising Priceline.com and discussing Jesse Ventura's gubernatorial inauguration audio, the conversation shifted to professional wrestling, criticizing its increasingly sexual and violent content.  A listener's potentially offensive fax was mentioned but not read. The NBA lockout was discussed, with Opie expressing his frustration. The death of Jerry Quarry from dementia pugilistica was mentioned, drawing a parallel to the potential health risks in professional wrestling.  The show concluded with a discussion of the graphic and disturbing film "The Dentist," and a humorous exchange about *The Flintstones*.

A subsequent segment began with a nonsensical discussion about a previous broadcast interruption.  The hosts reviewed the weekend's top five movies:  *Prince of Egypt* (deemed "stupid"), *You've Got Mail* (compared to *Sleepless in Seattle*), *Stepmom* (featuring a Scott Muni cameo), and *Patch Adams* (criticized as terrible despite its box office success).  A substantial portion of the segment involved a vulgar and critical analysis of Robin Williams' comedic style, focusing on his perceived lack of substance and reliance on improvisation and audience reaction. They reminisced about his earlier work and a *Happy Days* episode featuring Fonzie, discussing the show's evolution and Jenny Piccolo's surprising appearance.  The conversation digressed into observations about crossing the street and the absurdities of Robin Williams' stage presence.  Richard Pryor and Eddie Murphy were briefly mentioned before returning to Robin Williams' comedic techniques and his use of a beard.
