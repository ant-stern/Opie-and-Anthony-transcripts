SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast began with a "F.U. Friday" segment. Opie and Anthony vented about children disrupting their Long Island Railroad commute during spring break, with Opie detailing his attempts to discreetly hide Penthouse magazine from them. Anthony alluded to a significant "F.U." he couldn't publicly share due to pending litigation.  The conversation then shifted to the alarming number of livery cab driver murders and a lack of public outcry.  They briefly mentioned Whitey Ford's cancer diagnosis before pivoting to upcoming WWF events, including Stone Cold Steve Austin's return, and a hypothetical legal question regarding prize money distribution in a race with an unexpected passenger.  They promoted "WOW" stickers available at Ranch One locations and their distribution by Psycho Mark outside the Brooklyn Diner. Opie recounted a visit to an auto show, noting the cars' similarities and the novelty of an infrared camera revealing women's bodies. They also discussed the upcoming playoffs and Stone Cold's stiff performance on SmackDown.

Spaz's Week in Review followed, starting with Spaz's visible distress over a Time Out New York live read error and the exposure of his family's address.  His emotional state worsened as he revealed a $47,000 debt, partly due to loans given to others.  Callers, including Larry (who recounted a "WOW" sign incident and bear sighting) and Steve (who played a game guessing the reasons for Spaz's debt), contributed to the discussion surrounding his financial troubles.  A song mocking Spaz was also played.  This segment ended abruptly before Spaz's review concluded.

The show continued with a discussion about the hypothetical dangers of a gay scoutmaster, evolving into a debate on the nature versus nurture of sexual orientation.  Spaz asserted that people are born gay. The conversation then turned to the Ramsey family's refusal to take a lie detector test concerning JonBenét Ramsey's death, with the hosts discussing the reliability and admissibility of such tests in court.  A substantial portion focused on Spaz's highly offensive and racially charged opinions about Cubans, particularly concerning Elian Gonzalez and Cuban baseball players.  He used offensive language and stereotypes, repeatedly advocating for Elian's return to Cuba, even suggesting violent outcomes.  Numerous callers reacted to Spaz's statements, some agreeing, others vehemently disagreeing.  Ben Stein called, hanging up after taking offense to what he perceived as a coded antisemitic remark ("chew down the price," later proven to be "chew" not "Jew," by Opie).  Stein later called back, engaging in a heated exchange with the hosts.  As a solution to Spaz's debt, the hosts decided to have him wear his Boy Scout uniform and stand outside the studio with a sign saying "Insult Spaz for Five Bucks."  The segment concluded with advertisements and further discussion of the Ben Stein incident and the hosts' views on celebrities.

A phone interview with Ben Stein followed, deteriorating after Opie's use of "chew down the price," which Stein interpreted as an antisemitic slur. Stein repeatedly accused Opie of racism, despite denials.  The argument escalated, with Stein hanging up.  The hosts then played the commercial to prove Opie said "chew," not "Jew."  A smoother, though still offensive, interview with Shane McMahon followed, filled with offensive jokes about his sister, Stephanie. They discussed wrestling events, injuries, and behind-the-scenes information.   Listeners called in with opinions on the Stein interview.

The final segment began with a discussion on the etymology of "chew down," leading to more offensive jokes and ethnic slurs.  Ben Stein's outburst was mentioned.  The focus shifted to the promotion of "Wow" stickers at Ranch 1, with reports of inconsistent availability leading to accusations of a scam.  Spaz's Week in Review continued, featuring Spaz's incorrect and offensive commentary on various topics, including the prostate gland's location, Rudy Giuliani's health, and his cancer theories.  A urologist called to correct Spaz.  Other topics included George Steinbrenner's use of cancer for sympathy, a "Houdini hooker" on LSD, Darva Conger's Playboy photoshoot, and mispronunciations of Regis Philbin's name.  Numerous callers reacted to Spaz and the show's content. The show concluded with a promotion for an event where listeners could insult Spaz for $5, and a reflection on the day’s events, including an acknowledgment of offensive statements and a partial apology.
