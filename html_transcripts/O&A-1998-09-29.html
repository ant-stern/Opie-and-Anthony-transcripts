SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show began with the introduction of 12-year-old “Skid Marks,” a listener whose musical parodies, including songs about the Titanic, Charles Grodin's toupee, and a track titled "I Like to Fart," were played on air.  Skid Marks was scheduled to appear as a guest the following day.  This was followed by the airing of the "Road Rage" segment, a collection of recordings filled with extensive cursing and vulgar commentary about other drivers and pedestrians captured during the hosts' commute.

Listener feedback then took center stage, focusing on the show's placement on listeners' car radio presets.  Several emails and faxes were read, detailing the hosts' weekend activities (Opie's pumpkin picking and fall foliage viewing, Anthony's winery visits), and a listener's attempt at promoting the show with a Montauk banner.  The hosts then discussed the planned (but ultimately not conducted live) interview with Reverend Al Sharpton, who was in Haiti assessing hurricane damage.  A satirical, fictionalized phone call with Sharpton was performed, during which he humorously blamed the hurricane on white people.

A heated exchange followed with a disgruntled listener who accused the hosts of racism based on previous on-air comments about Sammy Sosa and Washington Heights. The listener's anger escalated, culminating in a threat to have her husband call and complain.

Suddenly,  Diceman, whose birthday it was, made an unscheduled visit to the studio, causing a brief, chaotic interruption before being sent away.  The show concluded with a discussion of upcoming TV shows, including *Mad About You* and *Real World*, and the announcement of upcoming Aerosmith ticket giveaways.
