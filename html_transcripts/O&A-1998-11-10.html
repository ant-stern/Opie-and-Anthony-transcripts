SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show opened with the launch of a new segment, the "Tuesday Night Hummer Club," designed to spice up marital intimacy by encouraging wives to initiate sex with their husbands on Tuesdays.  The hosts fielded calls from listeners, including one from a man whose wife was likened to "Nurse Ratched," leading to a live on-air plea to his wife. She reluctantly agreed, albeit with stipulations.  The segment then devolved into a naming contest for the club, with "Clean Your Tubes Tuesday" emerging as a popular suggestion.  Listeners also submitted proposed oaths of membership, alongside accounts of their own participation and experiences with the Hummer Club.  This was interspersed with a bet on the longevity of Bob Hope and Joe DiMaggio, and a Brady Bunch trivia contest, which showcased Anthony's impressive knowledge.

A significant portion of the broadcast focused on arranging a “Hummer” for listener John from his wife, Sue, via a live phone call.  The hosts, using highly sexually suggestive language, manipulated Sue into agreeing, presenting it as a reward for John’s loyalty to the show and branding their actions as "Pimp Radio."  The hosts’ banter was heavily focused on the act itself, playfully mocking John's apparent desperation. The segment concluded with the successful arrangement and suggestive implications that the act was taking place, prompting further playful teasing before being abruptly cut short due to incoming calls.

The show's conclusion featured comedian Andrew Dice Clay delivering a humorous, sexually explicit oath for female members of the "Tuesday Night Hummer Club," followed by another live call to a wife, attempting to persuade her to participate on her husband's behalf.  Numerous additional listener calls and emails detailed their involvement with and reactions to the Hummer Club initiative.
