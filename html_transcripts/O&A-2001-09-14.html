SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show broadcast immediately following the September 11th attacks was a raw and emotionally charged response to the tragedy.  The hosts, Opie and Anthony, abandoned their usual antagonistic banter, instead expressing shared feelings of anger, sadness, and numbness.  They noted the unusual quiet in their studio due to the lack of calls and described the somber atmosphere in New York City, observing the prevalence of American flags and the unexpected civility between strangers.  They also commented on the absurdity of some post-attack events, such as people selling pictures of the burning Twin Towers and a woman wearing a "Terror at the Towers" T-shirt while searching for a loved one, prompting angry commentary about those profiting from the tragedy.  A brief comedic respite was offered by playing a segment of a poorly performed Rocco wedding bit.  Discussion included Al Sharpton's perceived lack of support for the police, President Bush's speeches, and Reverend Billy Graham's sermon, with Opie expressing discomfort with Graham's religious message.

Numerous callers shared their experiences. A New Yorker detailed being in the World Trade Center the day before the attack; someone from Philadelphia described the overwhelming support and donations being sent to New York; and “Psycho Mark,” a New York City police officer, provided live updates on rescue efforts and the massive military presence in Manhattan, including the President's arrival by helicopter.  Technical difficulties hampered an attempt to play a clip of Vince McMahon's post-attack SmackDown speech.  Other callers shared their reactions and updates on aid efforts in various cities.  The broadcast also included a caller, Melissa, who praised their coverage and recounted a disturbing incident of Arab Americans delivering supplies being assaulted.
