The Opie and Anthony Show opens with a discussion about a list of banned songs from radio airplay, sparking debate over the reasons behind the exclusions of classic rock and pop tracks by artists like Led Zeppelin, The Beatles, Elton John, and Bob Dylan. Bush and Bin Laden, further diverting the conversation.

Andrew Dice Clay calls in, initially expressing sympathy for 9/11 victims and proposing a 24-hour radio telethon. However, the conversation quickly turns humorous and off-color, with the hosts interjecting jokes despite Clay’s attempts to maintain a serious tone.  The discussion of the telethon, nicknamed the "Radio Thong" by Dice Clay, continues, fueled by sexual innuendo and crude humor targeting Jerry Lewis, Bill Cosby, and other celebrities.  A caller, Joe, disputes Clay's claim of originating the telethon idea. The segment includes a game, "What's in my Pants," featuring a female caller and studio guest Jamaican Mike, and numerous calls detailing sexual encounters involving Jamaican men. Jim Norton, expected as a guest, does not appear.

The segment concludes with the "Win Stockbroker Dave's Money" contest, which involves a woman exposing her breasts in the studio for money, resulting in Dave’s abrupt departure and the segment's end.
