SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony Show opened with comedic commentary on the pre-Thanksgiving travel chaos, utilizing amusing airport interview soundbites.  Anthony then proudly, albeit somewhat nervously, detailed his ambitious Thanksgiving plan: a 500-mile round trip to Massachusetts for Thanksgiving dinner with his in-laws, immediately followed by bringing them back to New York City for the Radio City Christmas Spectacular and his radio show.  The hosts extensively discussed the logistical nightmare and financial strain this entailed for Anthony.

A substantial portion of the broadcast was dedicated to recounting the previous day's bizarre incident involving listener Wilda from Astoria, who consumed a portion of Stockbroker Dave's vasectomy remains to win Aerosmith concert tickets. The hosts discussed the overwhelmingly strong listener reaction to this event.

The show then transitioned into the "Tuesday Night Hummer Club" segment, designed to encourage couples to improve their sex lives.  Opie's conspicuous lack of participation became the subject of much ribbing.  A female caller engaged in a flirtatious exchange with Opie, which took an unexpected turn when she revealed she also listened to K-Rock, sparking a contentious debate about the superiority of each radio station.

A satirical traffic report by a Charlton Heston impersonator followed, delivering dramatic, biblical pronouncements about the traffic congestion. This segued into a discussion about a Metallica concert, Opie's controversial assertion that Lars Ulrich is the best current rock drummer (and the resulting listener outrage), and the hosts' recent censorship by CBS Radio for playing Isaac Hayes' "Chocolate Salty Balls."  They contrasted this censorship with the previous day's "cannibalism" segment, and solicited listener suggestions for songs about "balls" for a planned montage.  The broadcast concluded with further discussion about their censorship, upcoming Dave Matthews Band ticket giveaways, sarcastic remarks about the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, and a brief mention of the Hunger-Thon's response to the show's content.  Music from artists like Jimi Hendrix, Aerosmith, Led Zeppelin, and Cheap Trick played throughout the show.
