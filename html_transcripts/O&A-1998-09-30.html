SHOW SUMMARY:

The Opie and Anthony show opened with the hosts, Opie and Anthony, debating the baseball playoffs, with Opie switching his allegiance to the Yankees after the Mets' disappointing season.  They then launched into a segment called "Piss Off a Texan," a game where callers attempted to make a Texan hang up within 30 seconds using only non-threatening, non-curse-word insults. The prize was Opie and Anthony merchandise.  A listener, Inez, was mentioned for having the show set as preset #1 in her car, despite her boss's disapproval, highlighting a previous "road rage" segment.

Next, the show welcomed 12-year-old aspiring comedian Skidmarks and his mother to the studio. Skidmarks was known for his song parodies, including a previously recorded "fart song" featuring actual farts captured after a chili-eating session.  While the fart song wasn’t played live due to its…unique nature, the show did play his song "I Like to Fart," with Skidmarks providing commentary on each individual fart.  Multiple rounds of "Piss Off a Texan" filled the airwaves with callers employing increasingly creative (and often insulting) strategies against Texan listeners.

Between "Piss Off a Texan" segments, Skidmarks performed several of his songs live, including a "Monica" (Chelsea Clinton) parody and a "Titanic" parody.  A memorable moment occurred when Skidmarks’ mother let out a loud fart into a microphone while building up to the “Titanic” song.  He also performed his "Charles Grodin Toupee" song.  The broadcast concluded with further discussion of Skidmarks’ mother’s impressive flatulence, additional "Piss Off a Texan" calls, and  banter surrounding the Monica Lewinsky scandal and its media coverage.
